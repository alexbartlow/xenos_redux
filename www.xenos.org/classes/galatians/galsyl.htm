Inductive Studies in Galatians
with Chris Lang		 previous page

Syllabus

Spring 2000

Class goals:

To develop a deeper understanding of the content, structure and meaning of the book of Galatians.

To gain mastery of the Inductive Study method.

To cultivate a love for studying the Word.

Class assignments:

Inductive exercises including in-class exercises (~18).

Memorize the passage or verse in Galatians which most succinctly states Paul’s main thesis.

Grading

Grading will be based on:

1) the percentage of assignments completed
2) your ability to master the Inductive Study method.

Week One:

Intro to Inductive Method:

Paragraph Study: 1:1-5; 1:6-10

Assignment:

Read Galatians twice; Acts 13-15

Paragraph Study: 1:11-2:10; 2:11-21

Identify theme passage

Week Two:

How to Read the Bible

Review Homework

Discuss Historical Situation:

Paragraph Study: 3:1-5; 3:6-14

Assignment:

Paragraph Study: 3:15-18; 3:19-24

Memorize theme passage

Week Three

Review Homework

Paragraph Study: 3:25-29; 4:1-7

Assignment:

Paragraph Study: 4:8-11; 4:12-20

Read background literature TBD

Week Four

Review Homework

Paragraph Study: 4:21-31; 5:1-12

Assignment

Paragraph Study: 5:13-15; 5:16-24

Week Five

Review Homework

Paragraph Study: 5:25-6:10; 6:11-18

Concluding thoughts on Galatians

Stein, "The Game of Correspondence—Epistles," in Playing By the Rules, pp.169-186.

Galatians

John R. W. Stott, The Message of Galatians from The Bible Speaks Today commentary series, edited by John Stott, Downers Grove, IL: Inter-Varsity Press, 1986.
