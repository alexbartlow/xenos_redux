Inductive Studies in Ephesians
with Chris Lang		 previous page

Syllabus

Summer 2000

Class goals:

To develop a deeper understanding of the content, structure and meaning of the book of Ephesians

To gain mastery of the Inductive Study method

To prepare a couple of teachings from your Inductive Studies

To cultivate a love for studying the Word

Class assignments:

Inductive exercises including in-class exercises. I recommend NASB for inductive studies.

How many times does the expression "in Christ" appear in this book? Why?

Grading

Grading will be based on:

  • The percentage of assignments completed
  • Your ability to show competence in the Inductive Study method
  • Consideration will be given to class attendance and participation

Week One:

Intro to Inductive Method:

Overview: Ephesians

Paragraph Study: 1:3-14; 1:15-23

Suggested Reading

Interpretation

Norman R. Ericson, "Interpreting the Petrine Literature" in The Literature and Meaning of Scripture, edited by Morris Inch and Hassell Bullock.

Fee and Stuart, "Learning to Think Contextually" and "The Hermeneutical Questions," in How to Read the Bible for All It’s Worth, pp.43-71.

Stein, "The Game of Correspondence—Epistles," in Playing By the Rules, pp.169-186.

Ephesians

F. F. Bruce, Ephesians, NICNT, Eerdmans: Grand Rapids, 1984.

Peter T. O'Brien, The Letter to the Ephesians, The Pillar New Testament Commentary, Grand Rapids, Mi: Eerdmans, 1999.
