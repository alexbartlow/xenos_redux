Promise oracles depict God's plan--His promise of salvation--as being universal to all of mankind, even cosmic, and eternal. They describe how at the end of salvation history, God will rule personally through his Davidic king from Jerusalem. This will be a time of salvation, peace, and abundance for those who yield to God, and of judgment for those who resist His rule.

Promise oracles are often combined with words of blessing. Look for breaks in the text introduced by words like "then," "and then," and "after this." (Joel 2:28)

Example: Joel 2:28-32; Jer. 31:31-37; 23:3-8; Isaiah 9:6, 7
