Dennis McCallum, MA
Contributor to several chapters in The Death of Truth. Dennis is a writer and Lead Pastor at Xenos Christian Fellowship in Columbus, Ohio. He is the author of numerous articles on apologetics as well as several books, including Christianity: The Faith that Makes Sense and Walking in Victory. His M.A. in biblical studies and historical theology is from Ashland Theological Seminary.

Donal O'Mathuna, PhD
Contributor for "Postmodern Impact: Health Care." Donal received his BS in Pharmacy from Trinity College, Dublin, Ireland, and his Ph.D. in Medicinal Chemistry from The Ohio State University. He also received an MA from Ashland Theological Seminary in biblical studies with a focus on medical ethics. Donal, an Irish citizen, was a top class distance runner who had high aspirations to run for Ireland. He came to a personal knowledge of Christ while studying at Ohio State. He is now Associate Professor at Mt. Carmel College of Nursing in Columbus, Ohio, where he teaches medical ethics and chemistry. Donal has co-authored numerous technical articles in chemistry, and his article, "The Bible and Abortion: What of the Image of God?," was published in July 1995 as part of the book, Bioethics and the Future of Medicine: A Christian Appraisal, a project coordinated at Trinity Evangelical Divinity School. He teaches classes on science and Scripture at Xenos Fellowship, and co-leads a home fellowship and outreach group. Donal lives with his wife, Cheri and two young children in Columbus, Ohio.

Jim Fidelibus, PhD
Contributor for "Postmodern Impact: Psychology." Jim received his B.A. in Philosophy from Don Bosco Seminary in Newton, New Jersey in 1974. He taught music, English and Biology at a school in New York before becoming a graduate Student in Theology at the Pontifical College Josephinum. He served as an alcoholism counselor while earning his M.A. in counseling from the Ohio State University and in 1988 he earned the Ph.D. in Counseling Educational Services and Research, also from Ohio State University. He is Director of Research and Program Development at Interact Behavioral Healthcare Inc. in Columbus, Ohio. Now a Protestant evangelical, Jim teaches Bible studies in his spare time.
