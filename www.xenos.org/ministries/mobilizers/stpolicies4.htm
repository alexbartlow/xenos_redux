Short-term Mission Trips within Xenos Christian Fellowship

Page 4—Team Leadership

Leading a successful short-term mission experience and watching God change people's lives is a very fulfilling ministry. It can be very rewarding to see people come home motivated to direct resources toward the expansion of God's kingdom. It is also a tremendous privilege to witness all of the answered prayer that occurs in making a trip come together. An effective leader will strive to serve the team by helping them prepare for the experience, process their reactions to the culture, develop as a world Christian, and keep their focus directed on the Lord.

Responsibilities of Team Leaders include:

  • Communication with Xenos and/or Field Leader for trip planning
  • Supervision of team recruiting and selection
  • Coordination of pre-trip team preparation, team building, and fundraising activities
  • Sound management of funds raised, in conjunction with XCF Accounting Department
  • Confirmation of logistical arrangements, including air transportation, lodging, documentation, inoculations, etc.
  • Oversight of daily schedule and assignments while on the field
  • Preparation of the team for re-entry
  • Encouragement of potential team leaders and missionaries
  • Debrief of the church upon return

The team leader should also enlist a group of prayer supporters as a first priority. There is much to do; much that takes longer than expected. And since there is a high potential for significantly changed lives, spiritual warfare is to be expected. Effective completion of these responsibilities will be very time-consuming and mentally taxing for any one person. Leaders should consider delegating different responsibilities to various team members or volunteers, and thereby create ownership, encourage personal growth, and develop future leaders.
