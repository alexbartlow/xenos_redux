Featured Speakers

Lee Strobel

Lee Strobel was an award-winning legal editor for the Chicago Tribune and a spiritual skeptic until 1981, when he received Christ after a 2-year investigation of the evidence for Jesus. Lee has written several books, including the Gold Medallion Award-winning The Case for Christ, widely regarded as a classic. Lee also co-authored The Unexpected Adventure with Mark Mittelberg. The Washington Post describes Lee as "one of the evangelical community's most popular apologists." In recognition of his extensive research for his books, Southern Evangelical Seminary honored Lee with a doctorate of Divinity in 2007. Lee's unique blend of insights and Biblical truth will leave you informed, encouraged and hungry for the truth of Jesus Christ. 

Mark Mittelberg

Mark Mittelberg is the best-selling author of the celebrated Becoming a Contagious Christian curriculum (co-authored by Bill Hybels and Lee Strobel), which has been translated into more than 20 languages and used to train 1.5 million people around the world. He has also co-authored The Unexpected Adventure with Lee Strobel. Mark was on the leadership team at the Willow Creek Association in Chicago for many years and is currently serving at the Institute at Cherry Hills, an apologetics ministry that he co-leads with Lee Strobel and Garry Poole. Mark and his wife, Heidi, have two teenage children, and live near Denver, Colorado.

Randy Newman

Randy Newman brings over 30 years of experience sharing the gospel on college campuses around the United States. He currently serves with Faculty Commons, Campus Crusades' ministry to university professors. As a Jewish Believer in Jesus, Randy writes with experience, wisdom, and humor in his outstanding books Questioning Evangelism: Engaging People's Hearts the Way Jesus Did and Bringing the Gospel Home: Witnessing to Family Members, Close Friends, and Others Who Know You Well. 

Dennis McCallum

Dennis McCallum is a lead pastor and director of the Xenos campus ministry. A graduate of Ashland Theological Seminary, Dennis has authored several books, including Discovering God, Walking in Victory, Members of One Another, and co-authored Organic Discipleship. Dennis and his wife, Holly, have three children.

Gary DeLashmutt

Gary DeLashmutt is a lead pastor at Xenos Christian Fellowship. A graduate of Ashland Theological Seminary, Gary is the author of Loving God’s Way and co-author of Spiritual Relationships That Last. Gary and his wife, Bev, also lead a Bible study for post-college singles near OSU. They have two daughters.

Jim Leffel

Jim Leffel is an elder, senior sphere leader and teacher of our Sunday night Central Teaching. Jim publishes essays on Christianity and contemporary culture, and speaks at university, missionary and church groups. He earned degrees in philosophy at the Ohio State University and Trinity Evangelical Divinity School. Before assuming his current responsibilities at Xenos, Jim taught philosophy at Trinity Evangelical Divinity School, Columbus State Community College and Ohio Dominican University. He and his wife, Linda, lead a home group and have two children.
