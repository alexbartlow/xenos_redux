Matthew \n 1:1-17 The record of the genealogy of Jesus \n the Messiah, the son of David, the son of Abraham:1 \n Abraham was the father of Isaac, Isaac the father of Jacob, \n and Jacob the father of Judah and his brothers. Judah was \n the father of Perez and Zerah by Tamar, Perez was the father \n of Hezron, and Hezron the father of Ram. Ram was the father \n of Amminadab, Amminadab the father of Nahshon, and Nahshon \n the father of Salmon. Salmon was the father of Boaz by Rahab, \n Boaz was the father of Obed by Ruth, and Obed the father \n of Jesse. Jesse was the father of David the king. David \n was the father of Solomon by Bathsheba who had been the \n wife of Uriah. Solomon was the father of Rehoboam, Rehoboam \n the father of Abijah, and Abijah the father of Asa. Asa \n was the father of Jehoshaphat, Jehoshaphat the father of \n Joram, and Joram the father of Uzziah. Uzziah was the father \n of Jotham, Jotham the father of Ahaz, and Ahaz the father \n of Hezekiah. Hezekiah was the father of Manasseh, Manasseh \n the father of Amon, and Amon the father of Josiah. Josiah \n became the father of Jeconiah and his brothers, at the time \n of the deportation to Babylon. After the deportation to \n Babylon: Jeconiah became the father of Shealtiel, and Shealtiel \n the father of Zerubbabel. Zerubbabel was the father of Abihud, \n Abihud the father of Eliakim, and Eliakim the father of \n Azor. Azor was the father of Zadok, Zadok the father of \n Achim, and Achim the father of Eliud. Eliud was the father \n of Eleazar, Eleazar the father of Matthan, and Matthan the \n father of Jacob. Jacob was the father of Joseph the husband \n of Mary, by whom Jesus was born, who is called the Messiah.2 \n So all the generations from Abraham to David are fourteen \n generations; from David to the deportation to Babylon, fourteen \n generations; and from the deportation to Babylon to the \n Messiah, fourteen generations.3 \n

\n \n

1 \n Here, at the beginning of the narrative, Matthew indicates \n that he is establishing Jesus as the Messiah and the fulfillment \n of the covenants of David and Abraham. Isaiah makes it clear \n that the servant of YHWH would fulfill the Abrahamic covenant \n and the messiah would fulfill the Davidic covenant.

\n

2 \n This entire genealogy is framed front and back with the \n claim that Jesus was Messiah. If this is the thesis of Matthew's \n narrative then we would expect many references to antecedent \n messianic and covenant scriptures in order to demonstrate \n that Jesus fulfilled the terms of the Abrahamic and Davidic \n covenants.

\n

3 \n Matthew named 14 generations for each of 3 epochs but the \n list isn't exhaustive, a fact Matthew must have realized. \n It is not clear, then, what he is intending when he draws \n our attention to his symmetrical genealogy.
