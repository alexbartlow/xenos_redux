Facilitator's Guide for Chapter 1:
\n Are we Ready?

\n
    \n
  • McCallum claims that Charles Darwin's Origin of the Species \n changed the course of history. Are you aware of any of these changes? \n
    • \n
\n
\n

- Increase in atheism and agnosticism

\n
\n
\n

- Growth of theories based on Darwinian principles such as Social Darwinism, \n Religious Darwinism and Nazism

\n
\n
\n

- Declarations that the Bible is unscientific

\n
\n
    \n
  • Has your personal life ever been impacted by the doctrine of naturalistic \n evolution?
    • \n
\n
\n

- Most people have faced doubt, difficulty in witnessing, and even \n persecution at school

\n
\n
    \n
  • Imagine trying to discuss natural selection with a group of Christians \n in 1865. Would they be aware of what was happening, or interested in \n the subject? Should they have been?
    • \n
\n
\n

- Natural selection would have been very difficult for lay people to \n understand at the time, but those who failed to learn what it taught \n were unable to resist its influence

\n
\n
    \n
  • McCallum claims the real problem is that \"once again, Christians \n aren't ready for a major challenge to the Christian world view.\" \n What would be involved in \"being ready\" for the onslaught \n of a new world view?
    • \n
\n
\n

Consider passages like I Peter 3:15 and Colossians 4:5,6. Consider \n that Paul was familiar with the false teachings of his day (Acts 17:23, \n 28). Would you accept someone's testimony that your view on something \n was wrong if it was clear that person didn't understand your view?
