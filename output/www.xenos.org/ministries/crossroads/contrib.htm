Donal \n O'Mathuna, PhD
\n Contributor for \"Postmodern Impact: Health Care.\" Donal \n received his BS in Pharmacy from Trinity College, Dublin, Ireland, \n and his Ph.D. in Medicinal Chemistry from The Ohio State University. \n He also received an MA from Ashland Theological Seminary in biblical \n studies with a focus on medical ethics. Donal, an Irish citizen, \n was a top class distance runner who had high aspirations to run \n for Ireland. He came to a personal knowledge of Christ while studying \n at Ohio State. He is now Associate Professor at Mt. Carmel College \n of Nursing in Columbus, Ohio, where he teaches medical ethics and \n chemistry. Donal has co-authored numerous technical articles in \n chemistry, and his article, \"The Bible and Abortion: What of \n the Image of God?,\" was published in July 1995 as part of the \n book, Bioethics and the Future of Medicine: A Christian Appraisal, \n a project coordinated at Trinity Evangelical Divinity School. He \n teaches classes on science and Scripture at Xenos Fellowship, and \n co-leads a home fellowship and outreach group. Donal lives with \n his wife, Cheri and two young children in Columbus, Ohio.
