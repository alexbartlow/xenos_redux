\n 		Education
\n In postmodern public education, teachers are no longer transmitters \n of information to children. Instead, teachers facilitate children \n as the children construct their own knowledge. Who are we to say \n that western science is better than the psychoenergetics (precognition, \n psychokinesis, remote viewing, etc.) of ancient Egypt?
\n
Health \n Care
\n So-called alternative medical techniques were considered \n pure superstition a short time ago. Not any more! Now, authors like \n Deepak Chopra and Dolores Krieger have brought Ayurvedic Medicine \n and Therapeutic Touch into mainstream hospitals and nursing schools \n with the help of postmodern rhetorical techniques.
\n
Science
\n Have you heard people claiming that Physics proves the whole \n world is interconnected? Have you heard people claim that quantum \n physics shows that the universe is not rational? Why are top rated \n movies like Jurassic Park and Dances With Wolves always attacking \n science and western culture?
\n
Psychotherapy
\n The existence of therapists suggests that they want to seek \n a better state of affairs for their patients. But who determines \n what constitutes a \"better\" state of affairs? Isn't this \n a values judgment made by one (the therapist) for another (the patient)? \n It's not clear what the basis for such a judgment is, when reality \n is that which is constructed in the mind of the patient.
\n
Religion
\n The new tolerance in religion means never questioning the \n propositions of another religious point of view. But wait! There's \n one exception. It's okay to censure any religion arrogant \n enough to think it knows the truth. These are the fundamentalists. \n They have to be stopped before they gain the upper hand and begin \n persecuting other religions again. The marginalized religions, those \n of    non-western civilizations, must be given a voice.
\n
History
\n We don't look for what happened in history any more. That \n will never be known, and besides, everyone's reality was different \n then, as now. No wonder we have today, Women's history, Gay and \n Lesbian History, Black History and Native American History. It's \n no exaggeration to say that in postmodern cultural history, each \n marginalized group had their own experience, their own reality. \n The goal of history is to give voice to the silenced, or marginalized \n minorities.
\n
Law \n and Government
\n The Critical Legal Studies movement is increasingly influential. \n This radical reading of the law sees all law as political constructs \n designed to hold down the poor, women, minorities, or those of alternative \n sexual preference. They think judges cannot be \"fair\" \n in any objective sense, and are therefore really only engaging in \n the theater of justice while pursuing their own agenda.
