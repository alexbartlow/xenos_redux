Prayer: \n Where the Real Battles Are Fought
\n by Andy H.
\n

\n

I've \n observed a lot of spiritual growth and ministry for Mulberry, the man \n I have discipled for four years. It is a privilege to see with my own \n eyes the fruit that is born from him. I never know when, how or by whom \n intercession was made on Mulberry's behalf back here in the States, yet \n I know that you are praying. Only the work of the Holy Spirit initiated \n by prayer warriors on the home front can explain many of the things I \n have witnessed.

\n

Still, \n I think I can point out a few benchmarks in Mulberry's spiritual maturity \n and some factors that might have mobilized many to pray:

\n
    \n
  • Spring \n 1996—Several newsletters were written, and a Xenos supporter came to \n China for a visit. About eight months later, Mulberry became a believer.
    • \n
  • Summer \n 1997—I returned to Columbus on home assignment and spoke about Mulberry. \n A few months later, Mulberry was successfully grounded in the local \n church.
    • \n
  • Summer \n 1998—Several newsletters were written. A few months later, Mulberry \n had led about five people at work to Christ and was conducting weekly \n Bible studies with them.
    • \n
  • Fall \n 1998—Another Xenos supporter visited China. Soon after this, I began \n to lose count of the number of people Mulberry had led to the Lord. \n
    • \n
\n

Now \n after three years, Mulberry has accomplished more ministry with his own \n people than I could have accomplished in a lifetime. But, the focus of \n this writing is to encourage all of you involved in prayer for Mulberry \n and for all missionary efforts. I think our shared ministry is similar \n to Peter's and the praying church recorded in Acts 12:3-16. The church \n prayed diligently in hope of results, while Peter witnessed God's response \n to those prayers. May it continue to be this way as you and I continue \n to co-labor, through Mulberry and many others, for God to grow His Church \n in China.
