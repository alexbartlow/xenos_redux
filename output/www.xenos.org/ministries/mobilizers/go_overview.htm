Page \n 2— \n Overview of Awareness and Preparation Process

\n

The \n arrow or pipeline represents 5 phases for missions preparation. The goal \n is to reach unreached people groups.

\n

To function in a Biblical way, the church must be involved in missions \n (Mt 28:18-20).

\n

Phase 1: Preliminary Interest

\n

Through teachings, and modeling by the leadership, some members in the \n church develop a preliminary interest in missions. For some in this phase, \n the burden will grow into a more serious exploration of their potential \n role in missions.

\n

Phase 2: Exploration

\n

When a Christian worker in XCF is selected to be on our Servant Team \n (deacon), he/she moves into a higher priority category for missions. The \n equipping, training, character development and ministry experience it \n takes to be a Servant Team member means he/she is well on the way to a \n possible career in missions.

\n

Phase 3: Preparation

\n

The elders/management team in conjunction with the missions staff target \n some members to prepare to GO to a Xenos field. People in Phase 3 are \n invited to attend World Team's assessment center.

\n

At any point in this process, people may decide to stop pursuing their \n interest in a missionary career. They can be used by God as missions active \n members through prayer, financial and other home-based support and mobilizing \n others to GO.

\n

Phase 4: RACE

\n

World Team's assessment center. Candidates are assessed in a variety \n of areas for necessary skills, knowledge and gifting. World Team may offer \n suggestions or requirements before candidates are released to begin partnership \n development.

\n

Phase 5: Appointment

\n

This phase includes final preparation, training, partnership development, \n accreditation process. At this point the candidates would be formally \n sent by the church to reach the unreached.
