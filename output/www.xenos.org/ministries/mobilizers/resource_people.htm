People \n Group Ministries and Research

\n

Accelerating \n International Mission Strategies
\n AIMS is a ministry that encourages, trains, mobilizes, networks and facilitates \n the world-wide community of churches, agencies, other organizations and individuals \n for strategic global evangelization. AIMS currently has 10 alliances, seven \n of them focused on the 10/40 Window. AIMS offers seminars that focus on training \n to become more missions minded, and a new seminar whose focus is on prayer \n and how to better pray for the unreached peoples of the world.

\n

Adopt-A-People \n Campaign
\n The AAP Campaign provides a helpful introductory packet of information on \n adopting an unreached people group, including “Seven Aspects of a Successful \n Adoption,” and a list of sending and service agencies participating in \n the campaign. Also produced by the AAP Campaign:

\n

Adoption: \n A Practical Guide to Successfully Adopting an Unreached People Group, \n Bruce Camp, editor, 52 pp. This manual gives practical steps for incorporating \n your adopted people group into the life of your church.

\n

The Vision \n for the Nations video curriculum: an 8- to 13-week Sunday School-level \n introductory mission course based on the Perspectives Study Program.

\n

Doing Your \n Bit: a 10-minute AAP introductory video (contained also in the Vision \n for the Nations curriculum).

\n

Global Countdown \n videos: India, Mexico, Cambodia and Central Asia; highlighting unreached \n peoples and adopt-a-people stories from each country.

\n

Adopt-A-People \n Clearinghouse
\n A clearinghouse for unreached people group adoption information. Can give \n helpful information and ideas on adoption. People group profiles for sale.

\n

AD2000 \n and Beyond Movement
\n Among many other ministries, tracks which unreached people groups on the \n Joshua Project 2000 list have been adopted or targeted by churches and mission \n agencies. AD2000's Adoption Guidance Program & Mission Resource Kit are on \n the web and the Light the Window CD-ROM. Highly recommended!

\n

Advancing \n Churches in Missions Committment
\n ACMC is a network of missions-active churches, through which your church can \n get specific, practical assistance tailored to your church's global outreach \n needs, including adoption. ACMC will help you mobilize your congregation, \n build vision and help you become strategically involved in world evangelization. \n Conferences, vision seminars, regional staff all very helpful. Most conferences \n include adopt-a-people workshops.

\n

The \n Antioch Network
\n A fellowship of local churches that share ideas and strategies for initiating \n church-planting movements among unreached people groups. Annual Gatherings \n (August/September) are an excellent place for churches to learn and network \n with others interested in the same part of the world.

Caleb \n Project
\n Caleb Project staff are knowledgeable and well connected in the larger missions \n community, and serve the Church in taking her part in the Great Commission. \n Ministries include speaking and teaching, publishing books and videos, research \n and advocacy for specific peoples. Caleb Project has a 30-minute video on \n adopting unreached people groups, called Ordinary People/Extraordinary \n Love: Building the Kingdom of God Through Adopting Unreached Peoples. \n Co-produced with the AD2000 & Beyond Movement, this video profiles churches \n that have adopted unreached peoples and how God has used them. CP also offers \n Keys to the Nations: Articles to Inform, Guide, and Equip Advocates for the \n Unreached (1998), a 250+ page notebook with more than 100 articles covering \n a broad range of issues related to understanding unreached peoples, establishing \n effective partnerships, facilitating people-group adoptions, maintaining security \n and fundraising.
