Personal \n Finance Workbook

\n

Financial \n Objectives at Different Ages
\n During their twenties, clients are usually encouraged to:
\n • develop habits of saving and investing
\n • prepare annual budgets with a savings component of 5 to 10 percent
\n • accumulate a down payment for a home and an emergency fund of approximately \n six months’ expenses
\n • develop a favorable credit rating
\n • begin an investment program with long-term growth potential
\n • establish a retirement savings vehicle
\n • determine insurance needs and obtain adequate coverage: disability, major \n medical, property liability, and if the client has dependents, life insurance \n
\n • have a will prepared and sign it (including a guardianship provision if \n the client has children)

\n

During their thirties, \n clients are usually encouraged to:
\n • budget and control discretionary expenses
\n • continue an investment program and consider both growth and tax savings \n
\n • coordinate overall tax planning
\n • contribute to tax deferred retirement plans such as: 401k, 403b, qualified \n corporate plans, and IRAs
\n • plan education funding for children. Consider savings, trusts, gifts, custodial \n accounts, tax deferred investments, and so forth
\n • update wills for changing family situations

\n

During their forties, \n clients are usually encouraged to:
\n • provide education funding for children and funding for other monetary support \n the client desires to provide for family members. Consider gifts, trusts, \n home equity loans, unsecured lines of credit, and so forth
\n • increase savings as family expenses decline
\n • contribute to retirement savings, including tax deferred retirement savings \n
