Personal \n Finance Workbook

\n

Biblical \n Guidelines
\n The following guidelines should help you better understand the biblical position \n of finances in the areas of giving, savings and debt. This is not a exhaustive \n list of guidelines. Take the time to read the passages listed and do some \n study on your own or with your spouse.

\n

1. God owns everything. \n We are stewards of His resources.
\n Psalms 24:1, \n Haggai 2:8

\n

2. God will provide for \n all of our material needs. We should trust Him and be thankful for the provisions \n He has given us.
\n Philippians \n 4:18-19, Matthew 6:25-34, Hebrews 13:5

\n

3. All economic activity \n (working, giving, savings, debt, spending, etc. . .) has eternal consequences \n and impacts our effectiveness in this life as well as the heavenly rewards \n we will receive.
\n Luke 16:10-13 \n

\n

4. Our motives and attitudes \n in the area of finances are very important.
\n Proverbs \n 16:2, Mark 12:41-44, 2 Corinthians 9:7

\n

5. Until the guidelines \n 1 through 4 are understood and acted upon, it will be difficult to implement \n techniques and further instruction.

\n

6. Because of the guidelines \n expressed in 1-3 above, we can be generous and distribute our resources according \n to God’s standards. God is then glorified and we are blessed. New Testament \n giving indicates that Christians should be sacrificial and consistent.
\n 2 Corinthians \n 8 & 9, 1 Corinthians 16:1-2

\n

7. The generous person \n will be rewarded and the stingy person will suffer the consequences.
\n 2 Corinthians \n 9:6, Galatians 6:7, Proverbs 11:25; 21:13; 28:20, 27
