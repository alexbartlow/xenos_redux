Why Create a Trust \n in a Will
\n If the child beneficiary is a minor, funds cannot be distributed until age \n 18
\n At age 18, child entitled to receive lump sum distribution
\n Hold funds in trust until child reaches a more appropriate age

\n

Problems with Testamentary \n Trusts
\n Ongoing supervision from Probate Court
\n Cannot control investments
\n Must obtain approval for distributions
\n Ongoing need for attorney or other professional help

\n

Durable Power of Attorney \n
\n Appoint someone else to “speak for you” in the event of disability
\n Need to keep them current
\n Problems with enforcement
\n Not ideal, but certainly better than nothing

\n

Health Care P.O.A. \n
\n Authorize someone else to make medical decisions in the event we cannot ask \n you
\n What if you and your spouse are incapacitated?

\n

Living Will
\n Advanced Medical Directive
\n Provide Comfort Care
\n If you want this option, you must have this document
\n Remove food and hydration if:
\n cerebral cortex stops functioning
\n terminally ill and slip into a coma

\n

Do you need a Living \n Trust?
\n Avoids Probate Court supervision
\n Greater control to trustee
\n Disability planning tool
\n Greater flexibility in making distributions to children
\n Save estate taxes Top
