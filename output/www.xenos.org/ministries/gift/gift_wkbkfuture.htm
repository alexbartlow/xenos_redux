Where \n Do We Go From Here?
\n Congratulations on completing your budget! By following the budget you will \n be able to achieve the goals you outlined earlier in the workbook.

\n

But, what happens if your \n situation changes? On the positive side, you could receive a large bonus or \n an increase in your pay. You could also receive a one-time gift from a family \n member or inherit a gift from a deceased friend or relative. How does a godly \n steward plan ahead for such events as these?

\n

You have four basic choices \n when you receive an unexpected increase in wealth:

\n

1. Return it to God’s \n work
\n 2. Reduce debt
\n 3. Save for future expenditures
\n 4. Increase consumption

\n

Sometimes the challenge \n of increased wealth is more difficult than the lack of funds for our life \n style. The Bible explicitly discusses the responsibilities of the steward \n who is rich in the present world (1 Timothy 6:17-19, Luke 12:48):
\n “. . . and from \n everyone who has been given much shall much be required; and to whom they \n entrusted much, of him they will ask all the more.”

\n

You should have a plan \n for what to do with unexpected gain in personal wealth. You and your spouse \n should prayerfully consider the options listed above as stewards. Without \n a plan you will default to the least desirable of all options.

\n

We suggest that each family \n pre-determine what level of income is enough to meet their needs, both current \n and future. There is a point where each family should be able to say . . . \n I give regularly to the Lord’s work and I have enough on which to live. All \n overage income could then be added to what you are already giving to further \n God’s kingdom.
