Ministry vs. Sanctification

\n

Christian workers need a clear understanding of the role God plays in evangelism,\ndiscipleship and other aspects of ministry. In general, there is a continuity between\nGod's role in ministry and His role in personal sanctification. In both, God supplies the\npower to bear fruit. And in both, we are to act in accordance with God's will with\ncomplete dependence upon Him to perform His work through us.

\n\n

Failure to understand or believe in God's role in ministry will always have undesirable\nresults. These results include arrogance during \"in season\" times, as well as\npanic, pushiness and discouragement during \"out of season\" times. On the other\nhand, reliance on God's role in ministry will promote thankful humility during \"in\nseason\" times, stable perseverance during \"out of season\" times, and\nconfidence in God's adequacy through us.

As in personal sanctification, learning to rely on God's power instead of our own power\nis a process which takes time.

\n\n

One of the main ways we learn to depend on God is by experiencing failure (see II Cor.\n11:30-33; 12:9,10). As we respond properly to these failures, we gradually learn to\nminister in dependence upon God's power.
