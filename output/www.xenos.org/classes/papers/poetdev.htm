Poetic Devices[1] 

\n\n

Alliteration

\n\n

Repetition of the same sound at the beginning of two or\nmore words in a series (e.g. \"Fields ever fresh and groves ever green\").

\n\n

Assonance

\n\n

A partial rhyme in which the stressed vowel sounds are\nalike but the consonant sounds are not (e.g. bike and light).

\n\n

Chiasmus

\n\n

An inversion of the second of two parallel phrases (e.g. do\nnot live to eat but eat to live).

\n\n

Hyperbole

\n\n

An exaggeration meant to emphasize a point rather than to\nbe taken literally (e.g. The whole world is in an uproar over this teaching. This is as\nold as time).

\n\n

Inclusio

\n\n\n

Personification

\n\n

The presentation of an idea or value through its\nexpression as a person (e.g. Wisdom calls aloud in the street, she raises her voice in the\npublic squares...)

\n\n

Wordplay

\n\n

A clever exchange of words, a malapropism[2]  or a pun.

\n\n
