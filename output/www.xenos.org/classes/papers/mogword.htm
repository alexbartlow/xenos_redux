By Dennis McCallum and Gary DeLashmutt

\n\n\n
\n\n

The Word is of crucial importance for spiritual growth. It is also, for many\nChristians, the most neglected means of growth.

\n
    \n
  1. Neglecting the word will always eventually result in a complete stunting of spiritual\n growth, according to I Cor. 3 and Heb. 5. These passages clearly teach that there is no\n such thing as a mature Christian who is \"not accustomed to the word of\n righteousness.\" In other words, no amount of prayer, fellowship, or spiritual\n experience can make up for a deficiency in the our understanding of God's Word.
    2. \n
  2. Deficiency in the Word affects our experience with other means of growth. For instance,\n anyone who is shallow in the Word cannot minister properly, or pray as they ought (Jn.\n l5:7)
    3. \n
  3. To receive the intended benefit from Scripture, readers must \"handle\n accurately\" the word of truth. In other words, sound interpretation must\n proceed application. Devotional readers may feel no need to do background study or\n careful exegesis, leading to improper impressions of what a text means.
    4. \n
\n\n

For these reasons, the Christian leader must teach clearly on the fact that learning\nthe Word is not optional in the Christian life.
