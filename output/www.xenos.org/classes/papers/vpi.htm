Definition

\n

Inspiration - \"theopneustos\" - God so moved the authors of Scripture\nthat the resulting product was the Word of God written, totally without error in the\nautographs, in every area including theology, history, geography, and science.

\n\n\n\n

Reasons for Accepting This View

\n\n
An Inductive case for verbal inspiration:
\n\n
    \n
  • Demonstrate the relative historical reliability of the gospel records.
      \n
    • Bibliographical test - Are there enough copies to reconstruct the originals?
      • \n
    • Internal test - Does the author disqualify himself by contradictions or known\n factual errors?
      • \n
    • External test - Do other historical materials confirm or deny the author's\n testimony?
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • Note the claims of Christ to Deity recorded in the gospels.
    • \n
  • Verify the claims deductively by eliminating unsatisfactory explanations (not covered\n here).
    • \n
  • Therefore, since Christ is God, His teaching on bibliology is authoritative.
    • \n
