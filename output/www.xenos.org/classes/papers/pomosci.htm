From The Death of Truth

\n\n\n\n\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
Traditional Scientific PrinciplePostmodern Critique
Scientific Progress: Science should be a quest for truth about the\n universe, ignoring all forces who want to deny truth and defend their superstitions.Science actually arrives at its \"truths\" in response to social\n forces both within and without the scientific community. Their periodic shifts in outlook\n come as a result of irrational conversions on the part of influential scientific leaders,\n not from systematic searches.
Scientific Objectivity: Scientists are supposed to be objective\n observers. They study nature by direct observation, indirect observation or through\n controlled experiments intended to rule out bias.Observations do not interpret themselves. They are interpreted by a mind\n and the biases of any particular person or group conducting the experiment. They also\n argue that human minds are affected by their culture and language to such an extent that\n the \"actual\" nature of things may be unknowable.
Scientific Rationality: Science is supposed to be rational.\n Scientists use inductive or deductive logic to:

  1. - outline a problem or question
    2. \n
  2. - interpret an observation

  4. - articulate the logical implications of an
    \n hypothesis and
    5. \n

- test an hypothesis

Some postmodernists question, not whether science is rational but whether\n rationality provides any real insights into the world. They argue that there is no such\n thing as reason in the sense Europeans use the word, as we have seen. Endnote1 All agree that the rules of\n logic only apply within a given cultural paradigm, or model based on a given\n language/thought system.
Testing Hypotheses: A hypothesis is one possible explanation for a\n phenomenon. Once an hypothesis is formed, it must be tested under conditions where\n falsifiability is possible. The hypothesis must predict the outcome of experiments.Some historians of science and psychologists argue that when scientists\n form hypotheses they lose objectivity. In a number of cases, scientists refused to see\n data that contradicted their current understandings. Endnote2
Handling Data: Scientists should not select data to match their\n hypotheses. They should try to discover whether their hypotheses really match all\n available data.Hypotheses do not simply rise up from raw data. Instead, they originate in\n the mind of the observer, who then imposes the hypothesis upon the data as a way of\n organizing it. How does a scientist decide which information is useful and which isn't? Endnote3
Beginning Assumptions: Although the beginning assumptions of\n science cannot be demonstrated by scientific methods, they should be accepted anyway as\n reasonable beginning points. Scientists aren't consistent with their own method unless\n they admit their own presuppositions aren't undeniable. Endnote4The beginning points of modern science can't be obviously true because\n many cultures deny them. These beginning points along with the questionable bases for\n building upon them constitute the basis for calling science a purely subjective exercise,\n telling western man only what he wants to hear.
Quantum Physics physics: Quantum physics have revealed useful\n models for understanding the movements of subatomic particles. However the findings in\n this area are tentative and in dispute.Mystical scientists have allied themselves with other postmodernists to\n charge that quantum physics prove the eastern mystical view of the universe--a universe\n that is interconnected, irrational and spiritual.
