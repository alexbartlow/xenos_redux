The Nations Around Judah and Israel[1] \n

\n\n

Judah & Israel's southeastern and southwestern borders\nwere hedged by the ancestral blunders of Abraham (Midianites via Ishmael), Lot (Ammonites\n& Moabites via his younger and older daughter, respectively) and Jacob (Edomites via\nhis duplicity toward his brother Esau).

\n\n

Ammon  \n\n

    \n
  • Descendants of Lot and his younger daughter (Gen.19:38; c.f.\n Moab); 
    • \n
  • Ordered by God to leave them alone during the conquest,\n Deut. 2 
    • \n
  • Located east of southern Israel
    • \n
\n\n

Arabia  \n\n

    \n
  • A general term for the nomads living in the deserts of\n Arabia[2]  or a general\n term for peoples living east of Israel (Judges 6:3). 
    • \n
  • Included the following tribes: Amalekites, Buzites,\n Dedanites, Harites, Ishmaelites, Kadmonites, Kedarites, Kenites, Meunim or Meuites,\n Midianites, Maamathites, Sabeans, and Shuhites.
    • \n
\n\n

Aram (a.k.a. Syria)  \n\n

    \n
  • Descendants of Shem, son of Noah (Gen.10:22-23) 
    • \n
  • Located north & east of Israel 
    • \n
  • Damascus was the capital city of the Aramean territory
    • \n
\n\n

Assyria  \n\n

    \n
  • An early kingdom of Mesopotamia; prominent from 900-600 BC\n  
    • \n
  • Bounded on the west by the Syrian desert, south by Babylonia\n and on the north and east by the Armenian and Persian hills 
    • \n
  • Language: Assyro-Babylonian (aka Akkadian) 
    • \n
  • Their history is very bound up in the history of Babylon
    • \n
\n\n

Babylon (a.k.a. Chaledea)  \n\n

    \n
  • Civilization can be dated in this region from 5000 BC 
    • \n
  • Located south of Assyria between the Tigris and Euphrates\n rivers and bounded at its southern border by the Persian Gulf 
    • \n
  • The city of Babylon is located in the land of Shinar\n (Gen.10:10); modern Iraq; According to Genesis the city was founded by Nimrod 
    • \n
  • The nation of Babylon waxed and waned in power from its\n inception until its destruction by the Medo-Persian Empire in 539 BC; it's final period of\n ascendance was between 626-539 BC.
    • \n
\n\n

Caananite (c.f. Phoenecians)  \n\n

    \n
  • Refers to the area west of the Jordan and Syria 
    • \n
  • The people called Caananites were those in this land area\n from various people groups including Hittites, Ishmaelites, Amorites, Edomites, etc.
    • \n
\n\n

Cush (a.k.a. Kush or Ethiopia)  \n\n

    \n
  • South of Egypt 
    • \n
  • Founded by Cush, a son of Noah
    • \n
\n\n

Edom  \n\n

    \n
  • Descendants of Esau (Gen.36:9) 
    • \n
  • Ordered by God to leave them alone during the conquest,\n Deut. 2 
    • \n
  • Located south and southeast of Judah
    • \n
\n\n

Ephraim  \n\n

    \n
  • One of the largest tribes of Israel...sometimes the title is\n used to mean Israel.
    • \n
\n\n

Egypt  \n\n

    \n
  • One of the ancient kingdoms of the near east; it's\n prominence waxed and waned from 3000 BC to 332 BC when it was defeated by Alexander the\n Great; up to the Greek conquest, it consisted of 30 dynasties. 
    • \n
  • Located in the north east corner of Africa 
    • \n
  • Bounded by the deserts of Sinai, Paran, Shur and Zin and the\n Red Sea on the east, the Mediterranean Sea on the North 
    • \n
  • It surrounds the Nile valley
    • \n
\n\n

Israel  \n\n

    \n
  • During the time of the division of the Hebrew peoples this\n term designated the northern tribes 
    • \n
  • The first king was Jereboam 
    • \n
  • The capital city was Samaria 
    • \n
  • Samaria fell to Shalmaneser V of Assyria, December 722\n  
    • \n
  • The contemporary status of these peoples is unknown although\n Revelation 7:7 indicates that 12,000 from each of the 12 tribes are sealed against the\n coming doom...Apparently God has not lost track of them.
    • \n
\n\n

Judah  \n\n

    \n
  • During the time of the division of the Hebrew peoples, this\n term designated the southern tribes of Judah and Benjamin 
    • \n
  • See notes on the tribe of Simeon 
    • \n
  • The first king was Reoboam, son of Solomon 
    • \n
  • The capital city was Jerusalem 
    • \n
  • Jerusalem fell to Nebuchadezzar of Babylon, 597 BC 
    • \n
  • Around 10% of the captured Jews or their offspring emigrated\n back to the land in one of three waves of migration under the Medo-Persians
    • \n
\n\n

Midianites  \n\n

    \n
  • A nomadic people generally located to the south and west of\n Judah 
    • \n
  • Sometimes lumped together with the Ishmaelites; Gen.17:20;\n Jgs.8:22-24; a people located to the south and east of Judah & Israel; the Bedouin\n peoples
    • \n
\n\n

Meunites  \n\n

    \n
  • A desert tribe of uncertain origin 
    • \n
  • Lived in an area around the southeastern part of the Dead\n Sea near the Edomites. 
    • \n
  • Their chief city was Ma 'an (not to be confused with Maon in\n Judah) 
    • \n
  • They harrassed Judah during the time of Uzziah, were\n defeated and enslaved to the temple priests then. They appear among the temple servants\n (Nethinim) after the exile (Ez.2:50, 8:20, Neh.7:52).
    • \n
\n\n

Moab  \n\n

    \n
  • Descendants of Lot and his older daughter (Gen.19:37; c.f.\n Ammon) 
    • \n
  • Ordered by God to leave them alone during the conquest,\n Deut. 2 
    • \n
  • Located east of the southern half of the Dead Sea
    • \n
\n\n

Philistia  \n\n

    \n
  • A people living in an area corresponding to modern Lebanon
    • \n
  • Phoenecia (a.k.a. Sidonians (Josh.13:6, Jgs.3:3) 
    • \n
  • Located on a strip of land between the Mediterranean and the\n Lebanese mountains
    • \n
\n\n

Simeon  \n\n

    \n
  • One of the tribes of Israel 
    • \n
  • This tribe bears mentioning in a study of Isaiah b/c many\n maps place it squarely in Judah and so the question becomes, if Judah consisted of the\n tribes of Judah and Benjamin, why is Simeon not included? 
      \n
    • This tribe and Levi, by means of deception, executed\n vengeance upon Shechem (Gen.34:25-31). This angered Jacob so much (34:30) that he\n predicted their descendants would be scattered throughout the land 49:5-7 b/c of their\n progenitor's violence. 
      • \n
    • Over half of this tribe was killed during the wanderings\n after the Exodus, It was not given a separate inheritance in the land after the return\n from the wanderings Josh.19:1-9; it was given several border cities in southern Judah.\n This group may have migrated or been absorbed into Judah...it is not mentioned as a\n separate tribe in Judges, Kings or Samuel; 
      • \n
    • 1 Chronicles 4:29 indicates that they lived in their cities\n until the reign of David. 
      • \n
    • During Hezekiah a group migrated to a region southwest of\n Bethlehem - Gedor and another group migrated to Edom (1Chron. 4:39-43) 
      • \n
    • In 2 Chronicles 15:9 and 34:6 the tribe appears to be lumped\n with Ephraim and Manasseh as typifying the Northern Kingdom v. Judah and Benjamin\n typifying the Southern Kingdom.
      • \n
    \n
