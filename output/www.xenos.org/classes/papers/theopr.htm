B. The Attributes of God

\n\n
    \n
  1. Omniscience - Knows all things actual or possible at once (Psalms \n 147:5; Isaiah 40:28).
    2. \n
  2. Omnipresence - Transcends all limits of space (Psalms 139:7-10).
    3. \n
  3. Omnipotence - Can do whatever He wants (Genesis 18:14; II Timothy \n 2:13).
    4. \n
  4. Sovereignty - He owns the creation and rules it, causing all of His \n creatures to depend on Him (Genesis 14:19; Acts 17:24-28).
    5. \n
  5. Love (grace) - He always treats His creation with love and an attitude \n of mercy (I John 4:16).
    6. \n
  6. Righteousness - His moral character is the definition of goodness \n (Mark 10:18; Job 34:10; Hab. 1:13).
    7. \n
  7. Immutability - Devoid of change with regard to His attributes or promises \n (Hebrews 13:8; James 1:17; I Samuel 15:29).
    8. \n
  8. Veracity - Speaks of both truthfulness and faithfulness (Hebrews 6:18; \n Numbers 2 3:19; Titus 1:2).
    9. \n
  9. Infinite - Free from all limitations (Psalms 90:2).
    10. \n
  10. Self-Existent - The ground of His existence is Himself - God is uncaused \n and exists by necessity of His own being, therefore necessarily. See \n divine name YHWH (Exodus 3:14; Jn. 5:26; Isaiah 40:18). Totally independent.
    11. \n
  11. Justice - Retributive only towards fallen man, remunerative toward \n unfallen creatures (Romans 2:1-16). _
