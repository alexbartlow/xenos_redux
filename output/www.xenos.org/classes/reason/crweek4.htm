\n\n\n

Application: the resurrection of Christ

\n\n\n
\n

Hypotheses: Jesus died, was buried and resurrected on the third day

\n

Evidence:

\n
\n

Jesus died

\n
\n

Account of Jesus’ death consistent with known Roman procedure

\n

Few question that Jesus lived and died

\n

Roman historians attest to the historical Jesus: Tacitus and Josephus

\n
\n

Empty tomb (Mark 16)

\n
\n

Burial account is consistent with known Jewish tradition

\n

Women’s testimony to the empty tomb shows early Christians were more interested in\n historical fact than compelling arguments

\n

Impossible to teach the resurrection without the empty tomb

\n

Early Jewish polemic against the resurrection claim presumes an empty tomb (Matthew\n 28:15)

\n
\n
\n

Alternative hypothesis: Conspiracy theory

\n
\n

Psychologically improbable that the body was stolen, because the disciples did not\n anticipate this event prior to its occurrence.

\n

Historically/theologically unlikely that the body was stolen, since Jews did not\n anticipate a killed and resurrected messiah

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

\"Ancient Judaism did not know of an anticipated resurrection as an event of\n history. Nowhere does one find in the literature anything comparable to the resurrection\n of Jesus. In no place in the late Judaic literature does it concern a resurrection as an\n event of history.\" Joachim Jeremias

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Resurrection appearances of Jesus

\n
\n

Appearance to women indicates historicity

\n

Appearance to disciples unexpected and not initially believed

\n

Appearance to Paul certainly not expected

\n

Presence of 500 witnesses (I Corinthians 15:6)

\n
\n
\n

Alternative hypothesis: legend theory

\n
\n

Resurrection was the central and earliest Christian belief

\n

Insufficient time existed to transform Jesus story into resurrection legend

\n

Gospels and other New Testament documents are factual in nature and consistent with the\n claim to have been written by eye witnesses within 30+ years of the event

\n

Resurrection knows no parallels to Jewish or pagan religion

\n
\n

Disciples testimony of the resurrection

\n
\n

Willingness to die for their testimony lends substantial authenticity

\n

Something extraordinary must explain the origin of the early church

\n
\n

Alternative hypothesis: hallucination theory

\n
\n

Disciples lacked the psychological willingness to believe the resurrection

\n

Witnesses were too diverse and numerous to make hallucination plausible

\n
\n

Conclusion: the weight of evidence supports resurrection hypothesis

Logic and biblical interpretation

\n\n
\n

Biblical authors frequently appeal to evidence and argument in contrast to\n other sacred texts that merely make assertions

\n

Example: 1 Corinthians 15:12-19:

\n
\n\n\n
\n
\n 12. \"Now if Christ\n is preached, that He has been raised from the dead, how do some among you say that there\n is no resurrection of the dead? 13. But if there is no resurrection of the dead, not even\n Christ has been raised; 14. and if Christ has not been raised, then our preaching is vain,\n your faith also is vain. 15. Moreover we are even found to be false witnesses of god,\n because we witnessed against God that He raised Christ, whom He did not raise, if in fact\n the dead are not raised. 16. For if the dead are not raised, not even Christ has been\n raised; 17. And if Christ has not been raised, your faith is worthless; you are still in\n your sins. 18. Then those also who have fallen asleep in Christ have perished. 19. If we\n have hoped in Christ in this life only, we are of all men most to be pitied.\"\n \n
\n
\n\n\n
\n

Structure of the argument:

\n
\n

Either a resurrection from the dead or no resurrection from the dead (12)

\n

If no resurrection, then Christ did not resurrect

\n

If Christ did not raise, then

\n
\n

Faith in him is vain (14)

\n

Apostles gave false witness (15, cf. 1-11)

\n

You are still in your sin (17)

\n

No hope beyond the grave (18)

\n

Christians are pathetic (19)

\n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Unstated premise: Christ did resurrect (see 15:1-11)

\n

Conclusion: there is a resurrection of Christian believers
