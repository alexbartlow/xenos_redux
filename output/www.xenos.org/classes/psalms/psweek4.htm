Week 4: Wisdom Psalms[1] 

\n\n

Overview

\n\n

Wisdom psalms[2]  are identified as such because of their\nsimilarity to the genre of wisdom literature found in Proverbs, Job, Song of Songs and\nEcclesiastes. This similarity includes similarity in literary devices as well as\nsimilarity in content.[3] \nWisdom literature in general tends to contrast the life choices made by those who reject\nGod (e.g. the foolish or the wicked) with those who embrace God's rule (the wise or\nrighteous). God curses those who reject him, while he blesses those who obey.  \n\n

    \n
  • Examples include: 1, 10, 12, 15, 19, 32, 34,\n 36, 37, 49, 50, 52, 53, 73, 78, 82, 91, 92, 94, 111, 112, 119, 127,\n 128, 139. 
    • \n
  • General format
    • \n
\n\n
\n

There is no general format for these kinds of psalms. They\n may extol God's attributes,[4] \n his creation[5]  or his\n law.[6]  They may\n express skepticism.[7] \n Regardless of the content, however, the goal of the psalmist is to argue for the\n superiority of God and his ways.

  • Obey through trust in God's promises (Gen.22).

    Structure of the psalm

    \n \n
    \n\n
    \n
      \n
    • The structure is less obvious than some psalms; it appears\n to be a collection of proverbs organized around the problem of evil and God's retributive\n justice. 
      • \n
    • It is organized as an alphabetic acrostic of 22 strophes.\n

    • The middle section reminds us of God's eventual retributive\n justice (vv.7-11 & 27-33) and the contrast between the wicked and righteous\n (vv.12-26).

    Get Wisdom

    \n\n

    How do we live wisely? That is, how do we take on and\nlive out God's perspective? Well, for one thing we have to understand what we're up\nagainst when we decide to bring God front and center - we don't want to bring Him front\nand center. 

    \n\n

    The Enemy Within - Prov. 5:22-23

    \n\n
    \n

    There is within each of us a nature, an impulse, an\n intuition that is hostile to God and that left to ourselves we cannot do & do not even\n wish to do the right things. These very passages teach that God can inject us with a new\n nature...one that wishes to submit to his leadership. Romans 7:24,25, 8:1-2 & Romans\n 8:9 

    \n
    \n\n

    Replacement Therapy

    \n\n
    \n

    When we delight ourselves in the Lord we can have\n fundamentally different goals, perspectives and base of power.

    \n
    \n\n\n
    \n
    \n

    Goal - Either I press forward with my natural\n desires for self-stimulation, acquisition and affirmation or I take on God's goals for my\n life - to enjoy and honor Him.

    \n

    Perspective - Either I base my core sense of myself\n on what I do or on who I am - a child of God in good standing.

    \n

    Power - Either I draw on my own resources to change\n or on the power of God.

    \n
    \n
    \n\n
    \n

    Gal.5:16-26 & Rom.8:5-17 calls this experience -\n walking in the Spirit.
