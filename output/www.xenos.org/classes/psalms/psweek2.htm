    Structure of the Psalm

    \n \n
    \n\n
    \n
      \n
    • Built around a refrain repeated three times (42:5, 11, 43:5)\n
      • \n

    This is a common theme in the Psalms...remembrance of God's\n past actions provides hope for current and future actions.

    \n

    Be gracious1 to me, O God,2 according to Your\n loving kindness; According to the greatness of Your compassion blot out my transgressions.

    		Because of your love &\n compassion - forgive me. honneni – the same root, h - n\n - n is used in Nu.6:25 as part of the priestly blessing; it is used frequently in\n laments (4:1, 6:2, 31:9, 41:4, etc.); he has no right to ask for forgiveness on any basis\n other than the one God offers – his mercy anchored in his love (Exodus 34:6-7;\n Ps.25:6; Isa.63:7; Lam.3:32; Lk.18:13; 1Pet.1:3).
    \n 2 Elohim
    \n

    Wash me thoroughly from my iniquity And cleanse me from my sin.

    		This makes it clear that the\n purpose of the sin sacrifice was to symbolize God’s forgiveness and restoration not\n to cause it.
    \n

    For I know my transgressions, And my sin is ever before me.

    		I realize how wrong I’ve\n been.
    \n

    Against You, You only, I have sinned1 And done what is evil\n in Your sight, So that You are justified when You speak And blameless when You judge.

    		I sinned against you and\n you’ve every right to judge me for it.Some theologians, after\n studying the use of the word and concept of sin, argue that we may hurt others but we sin\n against God alone.
    \n

    Behold, I was brought forth in iniquity, And in sin my mother conceived\n me.

    		I am a sinner by nature.
    \n

    Behold,1 You desire truth in the innermost being,2\n And in the hidden part3You will make me know wisdom.

    		Since you want truth in my\n core, you teach me wisdom. OR ‘since’
    \n 2 battuhot – the inner parts
    \n 3 satum – the inmost place (this has partial assonance with battuhot)
    \n

    Purify me with hyssop,1 and I shall be clean; Wash me, and I\n shall be whiter than snow.

    		Cleanse me from my sin.

    Vv.7-9\n is chiastic relative to vv.1-2 .

    \n ezob - used to apply the Passover blood (Ex.12:22), in purification\n rituals to sprinkle water (lepers, Lev.14; Nu.19) or blood for sin sacrifices (Lev.14:4-8;\n Nu.19:6-8, 17-21; Heb.9:19).
    \n

    Make me to hear joy and gladness,1 Let the bones which You\n have broken rejoice.

    		Restore me.\n Joy follows from God’s restoration (Isa.65:17-18); independent of all circumstances\n except for one – having a settled state of radical trust in the goodness and\n provision of God
    \n

    Hide Your face from my sins And blot out all my iniquities.

    		Look away from my iniquity and\n forgive me.
    \n

    Create in me a clean heart, O God, And renew a steadfast spirit within\n me.

    		Make me a clean man, loyal to\n you.

    Restoration to a spiritual man is a gift from God (Deut.30:6; Isa.59:21;\n Jer.31:33-34; Ez.36:26-27 {see also Jer.24:7,31; 32:39; Ez.11:19; 18:31; 2Cor.5:17;\n Gal.6:15; Eph.2:10; 4:24})

    \n

    Do not cast me away from Your presence And do not take Your Holy Spirit\n from me.

    		Do not cast me aside.

    At the rebuke of Nathan he must have thought back to Saul who was cast\n aside for rejecting the will of God (1Sam.13:14; 15:10,22-23; 16:14-23). Now, David had\n ‘despised the word of the Lord’ (2Sam.12:9,10,14). What would God do?

    \n

    Restore to me the joy of Your salvation And sustain me with a willing\n spirit.

    		Restore me and make me loyal\n to you and ...

    Interestingly, it is God’s salvation that produces joy.

    \n

    Then I will teach transgressors Your ways,1 And sinners will\n be converted to You.

    		...I will be your advocate\n toward other sinners. Many MSS have this in the singular, ‘your\n way’
    \n

    Deliver me from blood-guiltiness,1 O God, the God of my\n salvation; Then my tongue will joyfully sing of Your righteousness.

    		Do not destroy me and I will\n praise you. could mean the death penalty (Ez.18:12-13) for adultery or\n for murder in the case of Uriah (2Sam.12:5,13)
    \n

    O Lord, open my lips, That my mouth may declare Your praise.

    		Let me praise you
    \n

    For You do not delight in sacrifice, otherwise I would give it; You are\n not pleased with burnt offering.

    		Animal sacrifice does not\n please you or I would do it.
    \n

    The sacrifices of God1 are a broken spirit; A broken and a\n contrite heart, O God, You will not despise.

    		Humility and repentance are\n the sacrifices that you accept. zibhe from the MT could be zibhi\n - ‘my sacrifice, O God’
    \n

    By Your favor do good to Zion; Build the walls of Jerusalem.

    		Be gracious with Israel.\n Protect Jerusalem.

    Some suggest that these two verses are post-exilic additions to\n the Psalm. This is conceivable and would not do great harm to the text but it doesn’t\n have to suggest some breach in the walls of Jerusalem...the walls represent protection\n that David jeopardized with his sin (2Sam.24).

    \n

    Then You will delight in righteous sacrifices, In burnt offering and\n whole burnt offering; Then young bulls will be offered on Your altar.

    		We will sacrifice to praise\n & honor you.

    Sacrifices were appropriate in the context of a right heart for\n they symbolized substitutionary atonement and demonstrated submission to God.

    What are we to make of curses?

    \n \n
    \n\n
    \n
      \n
    • There is constant tension in the Bible between love of\n people and hatred of evil.
      • \n
    \n
    \n\n
    \n
    \n
      \n
    • Love is in the Torah (Lev.19:17-18; Prov.25:21)
      • \n
    • So is hatred of evil (Amos 5:14; Ps.109:6-20; Acts 17:30-31)\n
      \n Who is more dangerous and evil than an angry ideologue? Apathy about evil is worse and\n when the two coincide it, the results are disasterous.
      • \n
    \n
    \n
    \n\n
    \n \n
    \n\n
    \n
    \n
      \n
    • We must uproot selfish vindictiveness (Gal.5:15, Ja.4:13-16)
      • \n
    • We must love the enemies of God (Isa.48:9; 2Pet.3:9;\n Col.1:21; Rom.5:10)
      • \n
    • We must hate & resist evil (1Cor.10:3-5 & Eph.6:12)\n but trust God to take care of any retribution (notice in the imprecations the author\n doesn't hope to mete out retribution).
