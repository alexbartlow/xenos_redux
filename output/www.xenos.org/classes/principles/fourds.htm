The 4 D's of Xenos Community Development

\n\n

Mission Statement:

\n\n\n

The mission of Urban Concern is to work with individuals in the inner city to break the\ncycle of poverty and hopelessness. Urban Concern is committed to raising up leaders who\nwill play an active role in restoring their community.

\n\n\n

The 4 D's of Xenos Community Development

\n\n

This is an outline of the strategy by which Xenos Christian Fellowship, through Urban\nConcern, is participating in community development. It has four processes which when\napplied should lead to long lasting growth and change in an economically disadvantaged\ncommunity.

\n\n\n

1. Define the geographic \nboundaries of an economically disadvantaged community to serve.

\n\n

* Focus on specific area.

\n\n

* Designate names of streets which border the area.

\n\n\n

2. Describe the needs of \nthat community giving special consideration to their perceived needs.

\n\n

* Study the area's demographics and life situation.

\n\n

* Food, housing, clothing, education, safety, job training, counseling, spiritual\nneeds, etc.

\n\n

* Identify which people in the area to serve.

\n\n

* Survey residents and program's serving the area

\n\n\n

3. Distribute resources \nand services to that community from Xenos Christian Fellowship and other \nsources.

\n\n

* Resources/skills and services to match described needs.

\n\n

* Develop programs which allow people to build relationships

\n\n

* Establish criteria for interacting with other agencies.

\n\n

* Establish relationships with other agencies who wish to participate.

\n\n

* Create and use various ministries and programs to help distribute resources.

\n\n

* Educational help, budget counseling, job opportunities, legal help, relief (food,\nclothes, shelter), training of different types, etc.

\n\n\n

4. Develop responsibility \nin that community, especially among their youth, for continuing its \ndevelopment.

\n\n

* Target the youth

\n\n

* Use above programs to develop Christian community leaders.
