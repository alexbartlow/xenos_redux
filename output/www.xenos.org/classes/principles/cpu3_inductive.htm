\"Always do right. This will gratify some people and astonish the \n rest.\" - Mark Twain

\n
\n

Main point: Always do right.

\n

Supporting points:

\n
\n

Why? This will gratify some people.

\n

Why? This will astonish the rest.

\"7 Be patient, then, brothers, until the Lord's coming. See how \n the farmer waits for the land to yield its valuable crop and how patient \n he is for the autumn and spring rains. 8 You too, be patient and stand \n firm, because the Lord's coming is near.\" - James 5:7-8

\n
\n

Main point: Be patient. (v. 7)

\n

Supporting points:

\n
\n

How? Until the Lord comes.

\n

How? Like a farmer waiting for his crops to come in.

\n

Why? Because the Lord's coming is near.

\"2 Devote yourselves to prayer, keeping alert in it with an attitude \n of thanksgiving; 3 praying at the same time for us as well, that God may \n open up to us a door for the word, so that we may speak forth the mystery \n of Christ, for which I have also been imprisoned; 4 in order that I may \n make it clear in the way I ought to speak.\" Col. 4:2-4

\n
\n

Main point: Devote yourselves to prayer (vs. 2)

\n

Supporting points:

\n
\n

How? Keeping alert in it with and attitude of thanksgiving. (v. 2) \n

\n

How? Praying that God may open up a door for the word. (v. 3)

\n

Why? So that I may make it clear. (Vs. 4)

\"5 I have been reminded of your sincere faith, which first lived \n in your grandmother Lois and in your mother Eunice and, I am persuaded, \n now lives in you also. 6 For this reason I remind you to fan into flame \n the gift of God, which is in you through the laying on of my hands. 7 \n For God did not give us a spirit of timidity, but a spirit of power, of \n love and of self-discipline.\" - 2 Tim. 1:5-7
\n

\n
\n

Main point: Kindle afresh the gift of God within you (Notice that the \n main point is not at the beginning of the paragraph). (v. 6)

\n

Supporting points:

\n
\n

Why? Because Paul is reminded of Timothy's sincere faith, as well \n as that of his grandmother and mother. (Vs. 5)

\n

Which gift? The one that is in you through the laying on of my hands. \n (Vs. 6)

\n

Why? Because God did not give us a spirit of timidity... (v. 7)
