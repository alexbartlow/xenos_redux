by Dennis \n McCallum \n

Before considering how to motivate people, we have to settle \n two important questions.

1) What is motivation in the Christian context? What does a motivated person look like?
\n

\n

\n

The answer we give to these questions will absolutely limit \n and channel our efforts to motivate others. My suggested definition of motivation \n is:

\n
\n

People are motivated when they are convinced of \n the correctness and the urgency of Christian goals to the extent \n that they are eager to act, and keep acting, to reach those goals regardless \n of what others do or think.
