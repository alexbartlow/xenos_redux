What kinds \n of sin are serious enough to merit removal from fellowship?

\n
\n

Although we have no proof that the lists of sins calling \n for removal from fellowship (I Cor. 5:11; II Thess. 3:6-15; I Tim. \n 1:20; Titus 3:10) are exhaustive, we think we should generally reserve this \n kind of discipline for the kinds of serious sin mentioned in these passages. \n

\n
\n
    \n
    \n

    Sins listed scripturally as serious enough to remove someone \n include:

    \n

    1. Sexual immorality- I Cor. 5:11
    \n

    \n

    2. Covetous- I Cor.5:11
    \n

    \n

    3. Idolatry- I Cor. 5:11
    \n

    \n

    4. Reviling- I Cor. 5:11
    \n

    \n

    5. Drunkard- I Cor. 5:11
    \n

    \n

    6. Swindling- I Cor. 5:11
    \n

    \n

    7. Unruly life- II Thes.3:6
    \n

    \n

    8. Division of the local church- Titus 3:10,11
    \n

    \n

    9. False teaching I Tim. 1:20
    \n

    \n
    \n
      \n
      \n

      In addition, we believe that other sins not specifically \n mentioned may call for removal as well. Examples include:
      \n

      \n
      \n
    \n
    \n
      \n
      \n
      \n

      1. Various types of social violence
      \n

      \n

      2. Chronic sexual misconduct (pornia)
      \n

      \n

      3. Drug abuse
      \n

      \n

      4. Certain kinds of unrighteous divorce
      \n

      \n
      \n \n

      Things that do not justify removal\n from fellowship include:
      \n

      \n
      \n
    \n
      \n
      \n
      \n

      1. Ungodly marriages
      \n

      \n

      2. Ungodly dating
      \n

      \n

      3. Disobedience to church authority
      \n

      \n

      4. Disagreement with, or criticism of, church authority
      \n

      \n

      5. Sin committed before conversion
      \n

      \n

      6. Sin (even though serious) for which the person has \n repented.
      \n

      \n

      7. Sins of omission
      \n

      \n

      8. Other sins

      \n
      \n \n
      \n
    \n
\n

Procedure

\n
\n


\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Key points to remember when leading a disciplinary meeting:

\n \n
\n
    \n
      \n
      \n
      \n

      1. Lead thinking before the meeting

      \n

      2. Pray for restoration
      \n
      \n

      \n

      3. Encourage active, compassionate, but firm participation

      \n
      \n
      \n
        \n
        \n \n

        4. Those affected by the sin should be encouraged\n to participate.
        \n
        \n

        \n

        5. Try to resolve disagreements about the discipline\n before the meeting.
        \n
        \n

        \n

        6. Take a Biblical stand even if opposed.
        \n
        \n

        \n

        7. Involve sphere leadership if you expect conflict
        \n
        \n

        \n

        8. Senior leader leads the meeting
        \n
        \n

        \n

        9. If unrepentant, then ask the person to leave.

        \n

        10. If repentant, accept the person, but issue a warning\n regarding continued sin.
        \n
        \n

        \n

        11. If the person disagrees, then consider them as \n \"not listening to the church.\"
        \n

        \n

        12. Is the person capable of terminating the\n sin with the available provision (i.e., an alcoholic or drug addict\n has been \n offered help and support)
        \n
        \n

        \n

        13. Have a restoration plan ready
        \n

        \n
        \n
      \n
    \n
\n
\n

In some cases, people might be removed from fellowship even \n though they never met with the church. Cases where this could happen include: \n

\n
\n

1. The person refuses to come to the meeting of the church.
\n

\n

2. The person doesn't belong to a home church.\n
\n

\n

3. The person is flagrantly disruptive or too physically \n dangerous.
\n

\n
\n

After Removal:

\n
\n

1. Inform members of the outcome\n
\n

\n

2. Inform sphere leader of outcome

\n

3. Do NOT pursue discipline on someone who leaves fellowship!\n
\n

\n
\n

Returning to fellowship after removal

\n
\n

1. Normally, the sin has been successfully terminated

\n

2. Home church leaders agree

\n

3. Sphere leader contacted

\n

4. Restitution made
