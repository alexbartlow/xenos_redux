Anthropology in Isaiah

\n\n

Biblical anthropology focuses attention upon humans in\nrelation to God rather than in relation to the world. In this section we will concentrate\non what Isaiah teaches about biblical anthropology. However, the themes of this book\nconstrain us to look especially at the effects of sin and faith upon man.

\n\n

From Isaiah we are told that people are created beings [1]  with spiritual[2]  and physical features;\nthat they have free will[3] ;\nthat they naturally incline toward rebellion[4]  and that God will rescue some from the\neffects of rebellion.[5] \n

\n\n

In spite of God's sovereign right to rule his own creation[6]  and his desire for\nintimacy with people, they wish to be free of God - free to live their lives according to\npersonal preferences. This desire is intrinsic to human nature. Acting out this craving is\nonly possible when God distances himself from his creation,[7]  because the rebellious are terrified of God;[8]  they know they are guilty.[9]  Isaiah elaborates on the\nway that rebellion works itself out in a manner that provides real insight into God's\nperspective on us and our relationship with sin.

\n\n

Rebellion[10]  manifests itself in three kinds of\nurges: 

\n\n
\n

The desire to acquire (i.e. lust of the eyes) 5:8-10, 23\n  

\n

The desire to stimulate the senses (i.e. lust of the flesh)\n 5:11-12, 20, 22; 56:10-12; 57:5 

\n

The desire to exalt oneself (i.e. boastful pride) 5:16-19,\n 21; 10:8-19; 13:11 

\n

Homework Assignment

\n\n

Read and annotate chapters 56-66

\n\n

Memorize the following verses: 57:15, 64:4 and 66:2b

\n\n

The pattern of sin explicitly described in 1 John 2:16 is\nrepeated in many places in the Bible (e.g. see Mt. 6). Isaiah illustrates that rejection\nof God's leadership in favor of self-rule leads to such practices. How are you currently\npursuing materialistic desires, sensuality and dependence on yourself? How might you\nresist these tendencies?

\n\n

Prepare responses to the following questions:

\n\n

1. Read Isaiah 2:6-22. Notice references to arrogance,\nidolatry and trusting man. How are these human attitudes and behaviors related (c.f.\nCol.3:5)?

\n\n

2. List 5 features of God's nature that are described by\nIsaiah. Provide citations for each example you give.
