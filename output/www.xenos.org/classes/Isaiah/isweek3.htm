The Messiah[1] 

\n\n

What is known of the 'anointed one' was only slowly\nrevealed. The early references do not specify that they refer to a single character--the\nMessiah. The Anonymous Servant

\n\n

In many cases God's servant is Israel (41:8-10; 44:1-3,\n21; 45:4) and in a few cases Cyrus, but it is also clear that the servant is an individual\nwith a mission to Israel and the world (42:1-4; 49:1-7 and see the explanation for 52-53\nbelow). That the servant is also the Messiah is supported by the mission of the\nservant--to be an atoning sacrifice (Heb.9:11-10:18). However, it is important to realize\nthat the nature and mission of the servant was not intended by God to be clear at this\ntime (1Pet.1:10-12; 1Cor.2:6-9).

God is triumphant over the nations for his people\n (24:1-27:13)

\n
\n

The Babylonian creation epic includes an account of Marduk\n killing the sea monster Tiamat; Ugarit literature has a similar story of a sea monster,\n Lotan; Hittite writings have a similar story too. Apparently, what was common to each\n account is that this sea serpent created chaos and was constantly in opposition to those\n gods that created order. Biblically, the myth was picked up, not to vindicate it but to\n show God was sovereign over these chaotic events (Ps.74; Job 41).

A case study: Hezekiah's faith in God (36:1-39:8)

\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Sennacherib's Annals do not recount the horrible defeat\n described in 37:36 but what they do say is interesting--that he shut Hezekiah up like a\n bird in a cage.

\n

This confirms he did not take Jerusalem which is a very odd\n military decision in light of the devastating attacks he leveled against other cities and\n against Egypt.

\n

Also, though the plague is different[12] , the historian Herodotus tells that the\n army was plagued and thus Assyria had to withdraw.

\n

It seems likely that the effect of the retreating shadow\n was due to some localized refraction of light (2Chr.32:31) rather than reversal of the\n earth's spin.

\n

Merodach-baladan rebelled against Assyria from 721-710\n under Sargon, from 705-703 under Sennacherib and continued to foment rebellion in Elam\n thereafter. It's likely that his visit to Hezekiah was in 712/711.

\n

See 2 Chr. 32:31. Hezekiah still had the seed of trust in\n humans.
