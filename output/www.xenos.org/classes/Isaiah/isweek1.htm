The History of the Divided Kingdom to the Time of Isaiah\n(1Ki.11- 2Ki.14; 2Chr.10-25)

\n\n

When we examine God's actions in human history, they are\nbest appreciated in the context of his plans and thus his promises. It is for this reason\nthat this book can best be understood in the context of the Davidic and Abrahamic\ncovenants.

\n\n
\n

Gen 12:1-3 Now the LORD said to Abram, \"Go\n forth from your country, And from your relatives And from your father's house, To the land\n which I will show you; And I will make you a great nation, And I will bless you, And make\n your name great; And so you shall be a blessing; And I will bless those who bless you, And\n the one who curses you I will curse. And in you all the families of the earth shall be\n blessed.\" (c.f. 15:1-21 for the cutting of the covenant)

\n

This promise to Abraham that all nations would be blessed\n through him was almost 1400 years past at this time.

\n
\n
\n

2 Samuel 7:4-16 That night the word of the Lord came to\n Nathan, saying: \"Go and tell my servant David...(9) I will make your name\n great...(10) I will provide a place for my people Israel...so that they can have a home of\n their own and no longer be disturbed...(11) I will also give you rest from all your\n enemies...(I) will establish a house for you...(12) I will raise up your offspring to\n succeed you...I will establish his kingdom. (13,16) Your house and your kingdom will\n endure forever before me; your throne will be established forever.

\n

Psalm 89:3f; 26ff You said, \"I have made a covenant\n with my chosen one, I have sworn to David my servant, (4) 'I will establish your line\n forever and make your throne firm through all generations.' (28) and my covenant will\n never fail. (29) I will establish his line forever, his throne as long as the heavens\n endure. (30f) [discipline for his son's if they sin] (34) I will not violate my covenant\n or alter what my lips have uttered...(36) that his line will continue forever and his\n throne endure before me like the sun...\"

\n

Psalm 132:11-18 The Lord swore an oath to David, a sure\n oath that he will not revoke: \"One of your own descendants I will place on your\n throne-if your sons keep my covenant and the statutes I teach them, then their sons will\n sit on your throne for ever and ever.\".......

\n

Just under three centuries before Isaiah's ministry, God\n made a promise to the king of Israel, David[1]. He promised several things but one of the more\n startling was that David would be the father of a lineage that would produce Israeli kings\n forever.

\n

Three hundred years after the promise, at a time when the\n entire nation seemed to be spiraling toward oblivion and just prior to events that would\n make the most ardent believer begin to doubt, God reminded them of that promise and began\n to explain more fully what it meant.

\n

The salient features of this contract between God and David\n are partly unilateral and partly bilateral and include the following:

\n
\n

Unilateral features

\n
\n
\n
\n

David will be a very successful leader of Israel and will\n finally have peace

\n

David's son will be the next in an eternal dynasty of\n Israeli kings (2Sam.7:13,16; Ps.89:4,29,36; Ps.132:12)

\n

Israel will have a land of its own (2Sam.7:10)

\n
\n
\n
\n

Bilateral features - Any Davidic king that disobeys God\n will be disciplined by God (2Sam.7:14; Ps.89:30-33) even to the extent that a particular\n Davidic lineage may be disallowed from the dynastic covenant (Ps.132:12)

\n
\n
\n\n

Isaiah's Revelation: The Abrahamic & Davidic\nCovenants Will Converge Upon the Messiah

\n\n
\n

An Eternal \"Anointed One\" Will Fulfill the\n Davidic Covenant.

\n

Isaiah 9:6-7 For unto us a child is born, to us a son is\n given, and the government will be on his shoulders. And he will be called Wonderful\n Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace, Of the increase of his\n government and peace there will be no end. He will reign on David's throne and over his\n kingdom, establishing and upholding it with justice and righteousness from that time on\n and forever.

\n

Isaiah 11:1-10 Then a shoot will spring from the stem of\n Jesse, And a branch from his roots will bear fruit. And the Spirit of the LORD will rest\n on Him, The spirit of wisdom and understanding, The spirit of counsel and strength, The\n spirit of knowledge and the fear of the LORD. And He will delight in the fear of the LORD,\n And He will not judge by what His eyes see, Nor make a decision by what His ears hear; But\n with righteousness He will judge the poor, And decide with fairness for the afflicted of\n the earth; And He will strike the earth with the rod of His mouth, And with the breath of\n His lips He will slay the wicked. Also righteousness will be the belt about His loins, And\n faithfulness the belt about His waist. And the wolf will dwell with the lamb, And the\n leopard will lie down with the kid, And the calf and the young lion and the fatling\n together; And a little boy will lead them. Also the cow and the bear will graze; Their\n young will lie down together; And the lion will eat straw like the ox. And the nursing\n child will play by the hole of the cobra, And the weaned child will put his hand on the\n viper's den. They will not hurt or destroy in all My holy mountain, For the earth will be\n full of the knowledge of the LORD As the waters cover the sea. Then it will come about in\n that day That the nations will resort to the root of Jesse, Who will stand as a signal for\n the peoples; And His resting place will be glorious.

\n

[See also, Micah 5:1-5, a contemporary of Isaiah]

\n

A Servant of God Will Fulfill the Abrahamic Covenant (c.f.\n Gen.12:1-3)

\n

Isaiah 42:1, 6 \"Here is my servant, whom I uphold, my\n chosen one in whom I delight; I will put my Spirit on him and he will bring justice to the\n nations....(6) I will keep you and will make you to be a covenant for the people and a\n light for the Gentiles, to open eyes that are blind, to free captives from prison and to\n release from the dungeon those who sit in darkness.

\n

Isaiah 49:5-8 And now says the LORD, who formed Me from the\n womb to be His Servant, To bring Jacob back to Him, in order that Israel might be gathered\n to Him...He says, \"It is too small a thing that You should be My Servant To raise up\n the tribes of Jacob, and to restore the preserved ones of Israel; I will also make You a\n light of the nations So that My salvation may reach to the end of the earth.\" Thus\n says the LORD, the Redeemer of Israel, and its Holy One, To the despised One, To the One\n abhorred by the nation, To the Servant of rulers, \"Kings shall see and arise, Princes\n shall also bow down; Because of the LORD who is faithful, the Holy One of Israel who has\n chosen You.\" Thus says the LORD, \"In a favorable time I have answered You, And\n in a day of salvation I have helped You; And I will keep You and give You for a covenant\n of the people, To restore the land, to make them inherit the desolate heritages;

\n

Isaiah 50:6-7; 51:2 I gave My back to those who strike Me,\n And My cheeks to those who pluck out the beard; I did not cover My face from humiliation\n and spitting. For the Lord GOD helps Me, Therefore, I am not disgraced; ...\"Look to\n Abraham your father, And to Sarah who gave birth to you in pain; When he was one I called\n him, Then I blessed him and multiplied him.\"

\n

Isaiah 52:7 How lovely on the mountains Are the feet of him\n who brings good news, Who announces peace And brings good news of happiness, Who announces\n salvation, And says to Zion, \"Your God reigns!\"

\n

Isaiah 52:13-53:12 Behold, My servant will prosper, He will\n be high and lifted up, and greatly exalted. Just as many were astonished at you, My\n people, So His appearance was marred more than any man, And His form more than the sons of\n men. Thus He will sprinkle many nations, Kings will shut their mouths on account of Him;\n ...Who has believed our message? And to whom has the arm of the LORD been revealed? For He\n grew up before Him like a tender shoot, And like a root out of parched ground; He has no\n stately form or majesty That we should look upon Him, Nor appearance that we should be\n attracted to Him. He was despised and forsaken of men, A man of sorrows, and acquainted\n with grief; And like one from whom men hide their face, He was despised, and we did not\n esteem Him. Surely our griefs He Himself bore, And our sorrows He carried; Yet we\n ourselves esteemed Him stricken, Smitten of God, and afflicted. But He was pierced through\n for our transgressions, He was crushed for our iniquities; The chastening for our\n well-being fell upon Him, And by His scourging we are healed. All of us like sheep have\n gone astray, Each of us has turned to his own way; But the LORD has caused the iniquity of\n us all To fall on Him. He was oppressed and He was afflicted, Yet He did not open His\n mouth; Like a lamb that is led to slaughter, And like a sheep that is silent before its\n shearers, So He did not open His mouth. By oppression and judgment He was taken away; And\n as for His generation, who considered That He was cut off out of the land of the living,\n For the transgression of my people to whom the stroke was due? His grave was assigned with\n wicked men, Yet He was with a rich man in His death, Because He had done no violence, Nor\n was there any deceit in His mouth. But the LORD was pleased To crush Him, putting Him to\n grief; If He would render Himself as a guilt offering, He will see His offspring, He will\n prolong His days, And the good pleasure of the LORD will prosper in His hand. As a result\n of the anguish of His soul, He will see it and be satisfied; By His knowledge the\n Righteous One, My Servant, will justify the many, As He will bear their iniquities.\n Therefore, I will allot Him a portion with the great, And He will divide the booty with\n the strong; Because He poured out Himself to death, And was numbered with the\n transgressors; Yet He Himself bore the sin of many, And interceded for the transgressors.
