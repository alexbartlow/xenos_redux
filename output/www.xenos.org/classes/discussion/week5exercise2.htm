When summarizing what people share, you can often correct something someone \n said that was corny, or churchy, or insensitive to guests. The teacher should \n always be on watch for jargon and non-user-friendly language and immediately \n say, \"Oh so you're saying...\" with appropriate language substituted \n for the former. Of course, you have to be quick on your feet to translate such \n language quickly. Also, if a point was made that is too extreme, or unqualified, \n you may correct it by either adding your own qualifications or balance points, \n or calling on the sharer to do so. You also have to consider whether to disassociate \n yourself from what was said.
