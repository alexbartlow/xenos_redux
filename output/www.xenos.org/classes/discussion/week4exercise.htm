Ausubel's Stages of Learning

\n

David Ausubel identifies four stages needed in a complete \"active\" \n learning sequence.

\n
    \n \n
  1. Advance organizers are introductory materials, \n which provide the learner with a framework within which the particulars can \n be understood. (e.g. learning that there are such things as spiritual gifts, \n and what they are)
    2. \n
  2. Progressive differentiation consists of moving \n from general explanations to increasingly detailed accounts of differences \n and distinctions. (e.g. If the gift of knowledge means knowing things about \n other’s lives without them telling you, what is the difference between \n that and the gift of prophecy? Or the gift of discernment?)
    3. \n
  3. Integrative reconciliation is a process of \n carefully defining how the new material is the same as or different from existing \n known materials. (e.g. Explain how spiritual gifts are the same of different \n than spiritual fruits.)
    4. \n
  4. Consolidation means that there is insistence \n on mastery of basic knowledge before going on to new items to be learned.
    5. \n \n
\n

Using your best guess at what these stages might mean, write \n a series of questions that would apply these steps to a discussion on \"How \n to know the will of God\"
