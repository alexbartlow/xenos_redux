Introduction: There is an overwhelming volume of information\navailable about parenting on the market today; what can we possibly cover in five weeks?\nOur hope in BNG is to look at what is the essence of parenting.

\n\n\n

What are the essential attitudes, realities and skills?
\nWhat are the irreducible elements of effective parenting?

\n\n

It starts in our own heart: \n\n

    \n \n
  • Perspective:
    • \n
      \n
    • We need to see the whole life of our child before us, not just today’s behavior and\n today’s challenge.
      • \n
    • Many of us come here \"beaten up\" with guilt or fear; is God at work?
      • \n
    • God never expected us to be perfect – and neither do our children.
      • \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n

    \"Love covers a multitude of sins.\"

    \n \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
\n\n
    \n \n
  • Purpose:
    • \n
      \n
    • The reason a lot of us loose our way is because we forget where we are going. Both for\n ourselves and for our children, we forget what being human is all about.
      • \n
    • Parenting is not just about:
      • \n
        \n
      • Breast milk or bottle
        • \n
      • Spanking or not spanking
        • \n
      • Getting them to clean up their rooms
        • \n
      • Making them happy or successful
        • \n
      \n
    • It’s about helping them to be fully human: to be men and women of\n integrity, purpose and maturity.
      • \n
    \n
\n\n
    \n \n
  • Focus:
    • \n
      \n
    • Why don’t the \"6 steps\" work? When we focus on behavior instead of the\n heart of our child, we fail to train their mind and values.
      • \n
    • Techniques and resources for discipline will only be effective when we have reached\n their hearts and given them a vision and a hope for maturity.
      • \n
    \n
\n\n

 It is a journey, not just a skill: \n\n

    \n
      \n
    • It matters as much where we are headed as where we are today.
      • \n
    • Parenting is a developmental process just like growing up is.
      • \n
    • The choice of direction is ours; our past experiences influence us, they do not define who\n we are.
      • \n
    \n
\n\n\n

\"Until a person can say deeply and honestly, \"I am what I am today because of\nthe choices I made yesterday\", that person cannot say, \"I choose\notherwise\". Stephen R. Covey

\n\n\n

 It is a community effort not an individual one: \n\n

    \n
      \n
    • Human beings were never intended to parent in isolation
      • \n
    • As we move more and more from community to closed family units, we loose perspective.
      • \n
    • Parenting ability and child outcomes have become a measure of self, and issue of pride\n and an untouchable subject for outside input.
      • \n
    \n

\"When parents see their children’s problems as opportunities to build the\nrelationship instead of as negative, burdensome irritations, it totally changes the nature\nof the parent-child interaction. Parents become more willing, even excited, about deeply\nunderstanding and helping their children. When a child comes to them with a problem,\ninstead of thinking, \"Oh no! Not another problem! Their paradigm is,\n\"Here’s a great opportunity for me to really help my child and to invest in our\nrelationship.\" Many interactions change from transactional to transformational, and\nstrong bonds of love and trust are created as children sense the value parents give to\ntheir problems and to them as individuals.\"
