I. Essential core qualities: \n\n

    \n
  • Gratitude – \"Cornerstone of Integrity\" Why?
    • \n
\n\n
    \n
  • Because a lack of gratitude is often indicative of self focus or unrealistic\n expectations
    • \n
\n\n
    \n
  • Because a lack of gratitude robs us of the motivation to move ahead and drains us of a\n realistic perspective
    • \n
\n\n\n\n

Role model for gratitude: Solomon?

\n\n\n\n
\n

Solomon - had everything; easy life (his father had secured most of the kingdom),\n riches (legendary wealth) , power and fame (Queen of Sheba came to confirm the stories).\n 1,000 wives and concubines

\n
\n\n
    \n
      \n
    • He denied himself no pleasure or possession
      • \n
    \n
\n\n
    \n
      \n
    • His conclusion: life is vanity, \"chasing after the wind\"
      • \n
    \n
\n\n\n\n
\n

Why was he ungrateful for the tremendous wealth and power he had attained?
\n Solomon appears to have made a crucial error...

\n
\n
\n \n \n \n \n
  When we mistake our deep longings
\n   for relationship and significance for material need,
\n   we can never be satisfied.
\n \n
\n\n\n

Barriers to gratitude: Material Myths \n\n

    \n
  1. The Myth of Perfection - we reserve our sense of gratitude for those times when we are\n unequivocally happy and unburdened.
    2. \n
\n\n
\n
\n
\n

If my life has chronic or incompletely resolved problems, we think...\"I can’t\n be happy\" what a sad conclusion!

\n
\n

We have to come to grips with the truth that pain and happiness can coexist

\n

If I think that my life must be pain-free or struggle-free, I will not be grateful

\n \n
\n
\n
\n

2. Unrealistic Expectations (what is normal house, income, vacation,)

\n
\n
\n

If I think that I should have all that I want, I will not be grateful

\n \n
\n
\n

3. The \"I Deserve\" Myth (the most insidious material myth)

\n
\n
\n

If I think that I am worthy of more than I have, I will not be grateful.

\n
\n \n
\n
\n
\n

4. Possessions have the power to make me happy (the most blatant material myth).

\n
\n
\n

If I think that I will be happy when I get… (salary, house, etc), I will not be\n grateful

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n \n
\n \n \n \n \n
 
\n   Paradox of gratitude: it is gained more readily by simplicity than from excess
\n
\n
\n
\n

5. They won’t love me if I don’t give them what they want (the most enslaving\n material myth)

\n
\n
\n

If I think that they only love me when I give them things, neither of us will be\n grateful

\n
\n \n

Who is the real role model...? Paul of Tarsus

\n
\n
\n

Phil 4:11-12 \"Not that I speak from want; for I have learned to be content in\n whatever circumstances I am. I know how to get along with humble means, and I also know\n how to live in prosperity; in any and every circumstance I have learned the secret of\n being filled and going hungry, both of having abundance and suffering need.\"
