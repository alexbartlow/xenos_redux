Homiletics\n Workshop \n

\"Preach the Word; be ready in season and out of season.\" All\n walking Christians will find the need to communicate God's truths to\n Christians and non-Christians. This workshop helps to develop those\n skills or gifts for young Christians, and helps the experienced teacher\n expand and deepen their work in the teaching ministry. Whether you\n teach in a public setting, home group, or one-on-one, God can use this\n workshop to bring his Word into the hearts of your listeners in a more\n impactful manner.\n

\n Inductive Study Method
\n Inductive worksheet of Philemon

Application\n of Scripture to Today's Culture and People
\n Application Restrictions

\n Six Laws of Dynamic Bible Teaching
\n Typical Teaching Format
\n Preparing Your First Few Teachings
\n Romans 12:3-8 Teaching Outline
\n Colossians \n 2: 8-15 Teaching Outline
\n John Stott on Preparing Sermons

Presenting\n Your Teaching
\n Authoritative Speaking

Assignments
\n Two 10 minute original\n teachings on assigned passages
\n The\n teachings should be given as to your home group
\n

\n

Assume there are\n non-Christians present

\n

You'll be responsible\n not only for proper interpretation and application, but also for justifying\n both when necessary

\n

Limit your teaching\n notes to one side of an 8 1/2 X 5 1/2 sheet of paper

\n

Bring your own video\n tape.

\n

Due the second\n week
\n A copy\n of the inductive overview of the entire book of 2 Corinthians.

\n

Due the week of\n each teaching
\n Teachers
\n A copy of the Specific Study (6 questions) for your passage
\n A copy of\n the Homiletic Worksheet for your teaching
\n A copy of\n your actual teaching notes.

\n

Non-teachers
\n Participate\n in giving constructive feedback to each teacher
\n Fill out\n a Critique Sheet for each teacher and after the discussion, pass it to\n the teacher.

\n

Due the week following\n each teaching
\n A copy of your own evaluation of your teaching, after viewing yourself\n on video. Don't simply fill out a critique sheet for your teaching. Rather,\n interact with the feedback from the instructor and other students. How do you\n agree/disagree with their feedback? What other areas of strength/weakness can\n you identify? What would you do differently the next time you teach?
