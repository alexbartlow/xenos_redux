HOMILETIC WORKSHEET\n

\n

Passage: Psalm 142\n

  \n

Date: May 27, 1997\n

\n

Structural Context:

\n \n

In a collection of psalms by David - an instructive psalm

\n

Written when fleeing Saul (see. 1 Sam. 22 or 24)

\n 		\n

Passage Outline:

\n \n

vs. 1-2 Express feelings to God

\n

vs. 3-4 Affirm God’s presence

\n

vs. 5-6 Affirm God’s power

\n

vs. 7 Affirm God’s purpose and future provision

\n

Theses:

\n \n

Draw near to God in overwhelming circumstances and trust God\n to be in control and \"rescue\" you.

 \n

Antitheses:

\n \n

Avoid God during overwhelming circumstances and rely on self\n to be rescued.

\n

Goal: To teach my audience to ask God for help praying God’s\n way and for God’s things\n

 
\n

Introduction:

\n

Supply context of psalm

\n

Define overwhelming circumstances now

\n \n

Major Points:

\n

I.. Express your feelings to God honestly

\n

A. David’s way

\n
    \n
  1. Verbs - pour, declare, cry = deep honesty with God.
    2. \n
  2. Reason: God is personal = He listens and cares
    3. \n
  3. Result: established intimacy, able to proceed to next aspects\n of prayer
    4. \n
\n

B. My way (Antithesis of David’s)

\n

1. Withdraw from interaction with God

\n

2. Reasons: don’t want to admit vulnerability;\n don’t want to feel pain w/ acknowledging

\n

3. Result: not able to proceed to next aspects, loneliness,\n despair, autonomy

\n \n \n \n

Conclusion:

\n

Joy and peace are found in drawing near to God. David enjoyed\n seeing God’s provisions and protection. We can also.

\n

Illustration/Application:

\n \n \n \n \n

Non-Christian co-workers sabotaging a Christian

People slandering you

\n \n \n \n \n

Antithesis of a personal God - \"a life force\" = impersonal, \"a\n deist concept\" = uninvolved and unconcerned, Allah = to\n high and distinct to pour out personal complaints

\n

Illustration: A dog, when injured, retreats to corner and licks\n its own wounds.

\n \n \n \n

Application: Lean against instinct to self-protect during overwhelming\n circumstances -draw near to God.
