Six Practical Rules:

\n
    \n
  1. Interpret in light of the context of the passage.
    2. \n\n
  2. Interpret in light of progressive revelation (Hebrews 1:1,2).
    3. \n\n
  3. Interpret scripture in harmony with other scripture.
    4. \n\n
  4. Interpret the unclear in light of the clear.
    5. \n\n
  5. Interpret the \"spirit\" of the passage, not necessarily the\n \"letter,\" or the literalistic meaning, especially when the text is a literary\n genre prone to figures of speech or colorful statements.
    6. \n\n
  6. Interpret with dependence upon the Holy Spirit, allowing Him to teach you. Mark T\n or F for each of the following:
    7. \n
    \n
  • Proverbs 3:5 -- \"Lean not unto your own understanding\" means we should avoid\n approaching the Bible on a primarily analytical level.
    • \n
  • Since the Bible is \"living and active,\" the interpretation of a passage may be\n different for different people.
    • \n
  • Unless we approach God's word with a deep reverence for God and a passion to know His\n will for our lives, we may often get the wrong interpretation.
    • \n
  • If the rules of interpretation give one answer and the Holy Spirit shows another, we\n should choose the latter interpretation.
    • \n
  • We should pray before studying that God will enable us to understand the passage.
