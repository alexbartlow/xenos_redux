Homiletics Worksheet

\n

Clarify the Thesis: \n

    \n
  • Central purpose of passage:
    \n     What     is the central \n point to be considered from the text?
    • \n
\n
\n

Write down ideas until you mold an adequate \n and evocative thesis. All of the sub-points and illustrations should contribute \n to your central thesis.
\n State the thesis of the passage early and often in your teaching. Each sub-point \n must refer back to the main point.

\n
\n
    \n
  • Goal of teaching:
    \n Why         is the teaching \n of this passage important: biblical clarification, correction or defending \n an attitude, belief or behavior. Goal is not always stated, but impacts how \n material is organized.
    \n
    • \n
\n

Examining the passage: \n

    \n
  • Summarize. What is the historical, theological \n or literary context as it relates to your thesis?
    \n What background does your audience need to have before they understand the \n passage?
    \n What extra information would make the passage come alive?
    • \n
  • Explanation/Support. What are the reasons \n given in the text that support the thesis?
    \n How does the author go about making his point?
    • \n
\n
\n

Clarify important words/theological concepts.

\n
\n
\n

What is the passage saying or presupposing \n about key theological issues. Clarification should include illustration and \n antithesis where appropriate or relevant. This may also be a good place for \n group input.

\n
\n
\n

What did this passage mean to its original audience? \n

\n
\n
    \n
  • Application. Discussion questions to \n get at the practical importance of the study.
    • \n
\n
\n

What does it mean for us today in the local/universal \n church?

\n

As I was studying this passage, what occurred \n to me? How did it challenge me?

\n

How would my life be different if I acted on \n the basis of this truth? In what positive direction may God be calling on \n me to act?

Develop an Introduction \n \n

    \n
  • Story, illustration or discussion question: \n Why does your audience need to hear \n this? Motivate them to listen by establishing the relevance of the topic in \n their lives.
    • \n
\n
\n

What current event relates to the point of this \n passage?

\n

\"Imagine what it would be like if. . .\" \n (antithesis to main point or paint a picture of the historical situation)

\n

\"Have you ever noticed. . .\" (observation \n question to illustrate diverse opinions on the topic)

\n

\"I remember when\" (personal experience \n with topic)

\n

\"Have you ever wondered about. . .\" \n (unresolved questions in mind of participants)
