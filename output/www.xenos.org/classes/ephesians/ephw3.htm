Conclude Ephesians Background

\n \n

Situation: Why was this letter written?

\n
\n

The cult of Artemis was powerful in Ephesus and the surrounding areas. In \n the ancient world everyone worshipped to some sort of God in order to bring \n fertility, rain or safety. And in this kind of environment there was little \n assurance that your prayers would be heard or that you could appease the \"gods\". \n There is a futility in this kind of pagan religion that we are unfamiliar \n with. Are my offerings acceptable to the gods? Is it good enough? This kind \n of insecurity led some cultures to make the ultimate sacrifice in order to \n gain the favor of the gods, namely, human sacrifice. In Christ, we have confidence \n that we are at peace with God, that we are his heirs and that God has ultimate \n spiritual authority.

\n

The Ephesians lived in a culture of spiritual power struggles where magical \n incantations and spells were commonplace. One of the themes of Ephesians, \n not found elsewhere in the New Testament, is one of \"the spiritual powers \n in the heavenly places.\" Some of the very words Paul uses in the prayer \n in 3:14ff, and which appear nowhere else in the New Testament, have been found \n on documents in Ephesus describing magical incantations. Paul seems to write \n to instruct his audience of the power and standing they have in Christ that \n far surpasses any powers that exist in the false gods around them.

\n

Most likely this was a circular letter. It was carried by Tychicus and probably \n circulated around much of Asia Minor. For that reason, we do not see personal \n greetings and we do not see Paul addressing an issue specific to any one church, \n but rather issues that are of interest to a wider Gentile audience. Paul \n writes to shape their identities as Christians (O’Brien, p. 57), \n This is what it looks like to be a Christian. This is who you are.

Theology

\n \n

Define:

\n \n
\n

\"Naming\" in ancient Semitic culture described the essence \n of a person and also being able to name something implied dominion over that \n person.

\n

\"Every family\" describes all of humankind.

\n
\n
\n

\"Kneeling\" in prayer was not common. Denotes greater reverence, \n submission.

\n
\n \n

Meaning:

\n \n
\n

Paul is asking God to strengthen them inwardly and dwell in them so that \n they might be grounded in God’s love. He is asking that they be \n filled to the fullness of God; namely, that they may take on a state of completion \n or perfection that belongs to God. (See 4:24 and put on the new self which \n in the likeness of God has been created in right. and holiness of the truth.) \n This is an entirely passive prayer. It is a prayer that God might do for us--and \n He is able to do for us (vs. 20!)--not that we would act on some truth, but \n that we would understand and be filled. Paul wants us to be filled with the \n perfection that belongs to God. Paul is moving toward 5:2 Walk in love just \n as Christ loved you.

\n

Parallel:

\n \n

Application:

\n \n

Them:

\n \n
\n

Receive God’s blessing and direct the praise to God. You cannot be spiritually \n mature until you grasp the nature of God’s love for you. Paul wants his \n readers to grasp the dimensions of God's love, to understand the incomprehensible. \n How can we do this? We can't. It is something that God must do for us.

\n
\n

Us:

\n
\n

What makes us uncomfortable about this kind of prayer? Paul is praying that \n God would basically do it all for them, that they would be strengthened, have \n understanding and be grounded in love and filled. You might be tempted to \n ask, \"where is our part in all of this? Are we to just sit there? What \n can I do? If you feel this way, it ought to tell you something about the way \n you approach God. We sometimes have a \"pull yourself up by your bootstraps: \n mentality about our spiritual lives.

\n
\n
\n

If you have this kind of attitude about your spiritual life, what are you \n communicating to those who look up to you as a more mature believer We need \n to intercede before God on behalf of those for whom we have spiritual responsibility. \n If God has given you a ministry of serving other people, you have a responsibility \n for them. Because of this responsibility, you need to keep them in prayer. \n Pray for a deepening understanding of God’s love… Tell people that \n you pray for them. Paul has told the Ephesians that he prays and he has told \n them what he prays.

\n

Paul’s prayer is not that they understand so that they can do, themselves. \n It is a prayer that God would do, that God would strengthen, \n dwell, and ground them in love (God’s love, not our love).

\n

Recap:

\n \n
\n

This incredible power and love of God, His ability to fill us and \n root us in His love is the basis for the remainder of the book. It is the \n basis for Paul’s ability to suffer on their behalf and the basis on which \n we can obey Paul's commands. We must read the rest of the book with our eyes \n on this passage, 3:14-21.

\"Put on the new self\"

\n \n

This concept of putting off and on garments was well known amongst the mystery \n religions of the day. A mystery religion was like a Masonic order in which \n the neophyte was inducted into a group by secret rituals and teachings. As \n a symbol of one’s new association, a fresh garment or uniform was donned. \n Paul may be evoking some of the imagery from their past in order to explain \n to them the radical break that has occurred with their old lifestyle.

\n
\n \n

Meaning:

\n \n
\n

Paul is picking up his thought from 4:1, what does it mean to walk in a manner \n worthy of the Lord?

\n

It means turning from the old life-style characterized by futility and darkness \n and leading inexorably to corruption and death. It is a life apart from God. \n It is a hardness to God. But walk instead as you have been taught by renewing \n your mind in the Spirit. This is who you are- created like God in righteousness \n and truth which is holy.

\n
\n \n

Until you come to grips with the blackness of your flesh nature and your \n hostility toward God, you will not grow spiritually (beyond a certain point.) \n It is only when we begin to see how black our hearts are that we understand \n the extent of God’s grace in our lives.

One of the implications here is that we’re only given two options: old/new, \n flesh/spirit.

Election: What does it mean?

\n
\n
\n

The church is God’s elect in the same way that Israel was God’s \n elect. God didn’t choose specific individuals to be Israelites, nor did \n he choose us to be Christians. He chose the church and he predestined her \n to be with Christ.
