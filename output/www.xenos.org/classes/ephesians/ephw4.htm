\n \n

Define:

\n \n
\n

\"unwholesome\"

\n

\"sealed\"

Meaning:

\n \n
\n

Because we have been recreated by God in truth and righteousness, we are \n to speak truth to one another. Beyond that we are to resolve our anger, share \n with each other, encourage and forgive. These qualities tell us about the \n nature of God. As we practice these things, we begin to be imitators of God. \n The paragraph culminates with an admonition to forgive as God has forgiven \n us (4:32), this is the quintessential nature of God and it is how we are to \n imitate Him.

Claim:

Do not walk in darkness but light

\n

Why would we not stop with vs. 7 as the thesis? We have a conclusion indicator \n and an imperative. Because verse 8c carries on the thought begun in vs. 7 \n and we’ve seen a consistent pattern in Ephesians toward \"not…but\" \n statements with the emphasis almost always being laid on the \"but\" \n half of the sentence. Also, 8c summarizes the content of the paragraph better \n than does vs. 7. The paragraph encompasses walking in the light.

\n

I think what Paul is saying is that by speaking \n the truth of Christ, or the light of Christ, that we expose people to the \n truth. In exposing people to the truth, we shed light on the darkness of their \n lives. As we speak the truth of Christ, we are calling on them to awaken \n from their dead state, and respond to Christ by rising from the dead. \n The effect of this is that Christ shines in us by transforming our \n lives.

\n \n

\n

One of the ways he wants us to live is according to His Spirit. This kind \n of lifestyle is characterized by edifying speech to each other and gratitude \n to God. You know if you are a negative person with a dour attitude or a razor \n sharp tongue, you need to be transformed. If this is you, you’re not \n walking carefully.

Principles for Application:

\n

2 Tim 3:16 Inspiration naturally \n results in application. \n \n

    \n
  1. What was expected of original audience? (Refer to your \n Inductive)
    2. \n
  2. Base application on correlation with original audience. \n How similar/dissimilar is our situation from that of the original audience? \n If dissimilar is there a principle that applies? Muslim culture without drinking. \n Hash smokers!)
    3. \n
  3. Seek out the intended principle. Distinguish between \n a principle and its application within the NT (Eph. 5:18) A principle is usually \n spelled out clearly in other passages. Therefore a biblical principle usually \n should be sustained by more than one passage.
    4. \n
  4. Determine what is normative for today: examples must \n be taught explicitly elsewhere to be normative. (I wish you were as I am, \n unmarried.)
    5. \n
  5. Write a specific action response. Apply in terms \n of relationships: God Satan, others, self, world. Be specific not general \n in terms of people and time frame.
    6. \n
  6. Pray and depend on the Holy Spirit.
    7. \n

    \n
  1. Epistles are occasional. Many specific passages are not relevant today (Paul’s \n cloak)
    2. \n
  2. Is the command, principle repeated? Note biblical consistencies and differences: \n uniform stance on love, theft, adultery versus a woman’s role in ministry.

  3. If the cultural setting is dissimilar, is there a principle that applies?

Theology

\n

Define:

\n
\n

\"Submit\" upotassw literally means to \"arrange \n under,\" \"to place under,\" to \"subordinate\" or to \n \"order oneself under\". It usually has a hierarchical connotation \n such as soldiers in an army to one of superior rank, (O’Brien, p.399). \n

\n
\n
