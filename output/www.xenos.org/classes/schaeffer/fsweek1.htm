The what and why of apologetics

\n\n\n

Personal Integrity and Stewardship of the Truth

\n\n\n

\"Such answers are necessary in the first place for\nmyself as a Christian if I am gong to maintain my intellectual integrity, and if I am to\nkeep united my personal, devotional and intellectual life.\" (151).

\n\n

Intellectual and Spiritual Work Together

\n\n\n

\"So then, the defense, for myself and those for whom I\nam responsible, must be a conscious defense. We cannot assume that because we are\nChristians in the full biblical sense, and indwelt by the Holy Spirit, automatically we\nshall be free for the influence of what surrounds us. The Holy Spirit can do what He\nwill, but the Bible does not separate his work from knowledge; nor does the work of\nthe Holy Spirit remove our responsibility as parents, pastors, evangelists, missionaries\nor teachers.\" (152).

\n\n

A Key to Discipleship

\n\n\n

\"It is unreasonable to expect people of the next\ngeneration in any age to continue in the historic Christian position, unless they are\nhelped to see where arguments and connotations directed against Christianity and against\nthem as Christians, by their generation, are fallacious. We must prepare Christian young\npeople to face the monolithic twentieth-century culture by teaching them what the\nparticular attack in our generation is, in contrast to the attacks of previous\ngenerations.\"

\n\n

Communication of the Faith and Spiritual Reality

\n\n

\"The purpose of apologetics is not just to win an\nargument or a discussion, but that the people with whom we are in contact may become\nChristians and then live under the Lordship of Christ in the whole spectrum of life.\"\n(153).\n\n
