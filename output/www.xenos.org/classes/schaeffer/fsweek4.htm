\"At the one-word level, the words learned most readily\n are those that refer to objects and events already well-established in prelinguistic\n intentionality. The word becomes attached as additional property, a kind of\n ‘handle’ to the already existing prototype in terms of which the object has been\n conceptualized. The fact that these words refer to things already known explains the\n startling rapidity of the growth of the child’s vocabulary. . . Beneath the welter of\n lifeworld (worldview) perspectives and the differences among individuals who share the\n same perspective, there is a level of thought and interaction on which people everywhere\n have pretty much the same outlook.
