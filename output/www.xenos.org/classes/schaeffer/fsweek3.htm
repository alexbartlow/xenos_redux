The Posthuman \n\n

\n\n
    \n \n
  • Human nature
    • \n
      \n
    • Product of Darwinian evolution
      • \n
    • Self is a social construct, thus it is possible to\n \"reinvent\" one’s self by artificial evolution. Computers and biotechnology\n make this possible..
      • \n
    • There is no correct or true model of the self.
      • \n
    • Postmodern era is a period of disintegrated selves.
      • \n
    \n
\n\n
    \n
  • Reality
    • \n
      \n
    • There is a \"multiplicity of possible realities.\"
      • \n
    • Reality is rooted in perception.
      • \n
    • Reality appears to be increasingly irrational.
      • \n
    \n
\n\n
    \n
  • Values
    • \n
      \n
    • Should we take control of our bodies? Is it right to\n artificially control evolution?
      • \n
    • Are genetic manipulations \"improvements\"?
      • \n
    • There is a sense that we are advancing, but not progressing.
      • \n
    • Okatu represent a new view of social interaction, relating\n to machines over people.
      • \n
    • Traditional norms of gender, sexuality and self-identity\n need to be explored.
      • \n
    • Will the posthuman era produce increased freedom or\n manipulation?
      • \n
    \n
\n\n
    \n
  • Truth
    • \n
      \n
    • Absolute truth no longer applies. There are numerous ways to\n look at the world, and numerous equally valid standards of behavior.
      • \n
    • There is little sense of past or future.
      • \n
    • Absolutes no longer apply in political and social contexts.
      • \n
    • New technologies lead to new structures of thinking.\n Deductive rational structures are eclipsed by electronic media’s compressed sense of\n time. Images replace deduction.
      • \n
    • Patterns of thinking are becoming less rational.
      • \n
    • Hierarchical belief systems are being replaced by\n perceptual, multifaceted ones.
      • \n
