Some more radical critics think that the \"Hellenists\" were a splinter group\nin Judaism before the time of Christ. Their \"anti-temple, anti-ritualistic bias seems\nclear from Stephen's speech,\" comments Trudinger in what appears to be a circular\nargument. There is little doubt that there\nwere more liberally minded Jews known as Hellenists, but it is not clear that they were\nagainst the Temple or ritual. The only extra-biblical evidence adduced is an alleged\nsimilarity to the spirit of the Qumran community. The parallels, however, appear\nsuperficial.

14The phrase \"ho topos toutos\" (this place) is found exclusively in Luke-Acts\nin the New Testament, (sometimes in other cases). Once it is found in and Luke (16:28),\nand five times in Acts (6:13,14; 7:7; 21:28 twice). In 7:7 it is clearly referring to the\nland of Israel (see below). In 6:13,14 it is likely that the first reference may refer to\nthe land of Israel, and in 21:28 it is also possible that the first usage refers to the\nland rather than the temple. Only when \"hagiou\" is inserted does it become\nlikely that the temple is being referred to exclusively, and even then there is room for\ndoubt as noted above in note 13. Also see Knowling for a brief explanation of the textual\nproblem with this phrase in vs. 13. R. J. Knowling, \"The Acts of the Apostles.\"\nin The Expositor's Bible Commentary, Vol. II p. 177. The variant does not affect\nthe sense of the charge against Stephen.
