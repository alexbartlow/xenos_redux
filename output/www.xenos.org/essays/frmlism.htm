What is it?

\n\n

Formalism refers to a tendency in religious thought and practice to shift focus away\nfrom the abstract, the spiritual, the personal, or the ethical principles in a religion\nand toward the outward forms that embody that religion. Outward forms can refer to: \n

    \n
  • the sacred buildings or shrines in which worship occurs
    • \n
  • the calendar of sacred days and times around which religious life is oriented
    • \n
  • the rituals through which followers can experience the sacred or be fixed in some way
    • \n
  • special dress, diet, language, or other behaviors unique to that religion
    • \n
\n\n

Why Not?

\n\n

Formalism is found the world over in all religions. It is also common in historical\nChristianity, but is roundly condemned in the Bible, both in Old and New Testaments.\nFormalism is an impersonal outlook which misses the point where the things of God are\nconcerned. It diverts people's attention and affections from the important to the\nunimportant--from the wine to the wineskins.
