36See a concise analysis of Asceticism based on the writings of Augustine in Henry\nChadwick, \"The Ascetic Ideal in the History of the Church,\" in Monks Hermits and\nthe Ascetic Tradition, pp. 1-23. This period was formative for the history of asceticism\nin the church, because the overwhelming influx of so-called \"converts\" after\nConstantine was threatening to erase the distinction between Christian and pagan. In the\nface of social pressure to become Christian, millions were joining the church without\nhaving been converted. 40Although Pius Melia claims that, \"It is therefore beyond doubt that, before the\ntime of Luther and Calvin, the Waldenses admitted all the books of the [Catholic] Bible. .\n.\" (e.g. including the Apocrypha) the only evidence he gives is the presence of two\nportions of apocryphal books in translation in the Cambridge collection of Waldensians\nmss. Pius Melia, D. D., The Origin, Persecutions, and Doctrines of the Waldenses,\np. 93,98. However, this conclusion seems unwarranted. The Waldensians definitely\nrejected the existence of purgatory during the early period, and claimed that it was\nforeign to the scriptures. They also argued that there was nothing anyone could do once a\nperson died, as can be seen from the following early record of the Inquisition.

\n\n

\". . .one who does good will go to paradise, and one who does evil will go to hell\nand damnation; purgatory does not exist. Indeed, whoever believes in purgatory is\ncondemned already. Further, charities after one's death should not be done, for charities\nafter death have no value; that they do not profit the one who does them if they are not\ndone before death. . .\" \"Interrogation of Filippo Regis\" at his trial in\n1451, published by G. Weitzecker, Processo di un valdese nell'anno 1451, in Rivista\nCristiana IX (1881) pp. 363-67, Partially reproduced in translation in Giorgio Tourn, The\nWaldensians, pp. 55,56.

\n\n

These views would be impossible to argue if the book of II Maccabees was viewed as\ncanonical. There is no statement in any Waldensian writing affirming the canonicity of the\nApocrypha. These books, like the writings of the fathers (which are also found in\nWaldensian translation) were apparently used as supplemental material, as indeed they were\nso viewed by most of the Roman Catholic Church during this period.
