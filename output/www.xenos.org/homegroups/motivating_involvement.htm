Leading a home group requires a great deal of commitment on the part \n of the leader. Visitors from other churches often wonder how we motivate \n people in Xenos toward this level sacrifice and service:

\n \n \n

Perhaps you are already personally convinced of the biblical \n rationale for having Home Groups and the need to be involved in one \n yourself. However, it can be quite another matter to motivate others to get involved.

\n

Most of the adults attending Xenos are also involved a home group. \n How do we motivate people toward this level of involvement? How do we \n communicate the importance of home groups to all who attend our Central \n Teachings?

\n

Here are some of the ways people are made aware of the benefits \n of being in a home group at Xenos:

\n
    \n

    Taken by their host

    \n

    Most new people at a Xenos meeting were brought by a member who witnessed \n to them. These hosts will also invite them to be their guest at their \n own home churches. Home churches grow relationally, not geographically.

    \n

    Modeling from the leadership

    \n

    Every Central Teaching teacher, every Xenos staff member, and every \n type of ministry team leader is involved in a home group.

    \n

    Teachings

    \n

    Central Teaching teachers and class teachers regularly highlight the importance of home group involvement \n for spiritual growth. They communicate this through direct instruction \n and through offering personal examples. This channel has been extremely \n effective in raising people’s interest in home groups.

    \n

    Spontaneous sharing in the larger meetings

    \n

    After the teaching portion of every Central Teaching we have a time \n for questions and comments from the group. Members will often spontaneously \n share about a spiritual victory, an answered prayer, or an experience \n of love that occurred as a direct result of his involvement with others \n in a home group. Others present can sense the intimacy available through \n home groups.

    \n

    Home Group Connection/Welcome Booth

    \n

    The Home Group Connection web page \n\n\nallows you to see where home groups are located around Columbus. Information includes meeting nights, demographics and an e-mail address for contacting a leader of each group.\n\nThe highly-visible Welcome Booth is located in the lobby \n of the meeting building. Here, curious individuals can interact with the home group section of the Xenos \n web site, and talk with Home Group Connection staff members.

    \n

    Values:

    \n

    Koinonia

    \n

    As mentioned elsewhere, true biblical koinonia develops best \n in smaller groups.

    \n
      \n
    • \n

      For one thing, smaller home groups provide a natural venue for \n building close, consistent relationships for giving and receiving \n biblical love. The home group context allows a profound level of \n intimacy to develop among the members, so that each person can truly \n play a vital role in the spiritual growth of others in the group. \n Home groups become even closer when they operate cell \n groups.

      \n
      • \n
    • \n

      Also, home groups provide a natural context for discovering and \n exercising spiritual \n gifts. People are more aware of each other’s needs in smaller \n groups, and opportunities to serve and step into new roles are most \n clearly seen in the context of a home group. Thus, home groups offer \n individuals an excellent opportunity to discover and then pursue \n God’s specific purpose for them.

      \n
      • \n
    \n

    Opportunity to practice what we learn

    \n

    \"Knowledge puffs up, but love builds up\" (1 Corinthians 8:1). \n God intends for biblical knowledge to be put into practice in the context \n of loving relationships. Home groups provide an ideal environment in \n which individuals can contribute to the meeting by sharing what they've \n learned (1 Corinthians 14:26), and they can use their growing biblical \n knowledge to apply truth with love to each others’ life situations.

    \n

    Discipleship

    \n

    One-on-one discipleship (or mentoring) relationships are common in \n Xenos, but normally only with people already involved in a home group \n and a cell group. Disciples can be coached most effectively in the context \n of home groups and cell groups as they learn to love, serve, shepherd, \n teach, etc.

    \n

    Xenos Classes

    \n

    Some Xenos classes are available only to home group members. These \n include Christian Principles, \n as well as all other courses requiring Christian Principles as a prerequisite.

    \n

    Ministry Opportunities

    \n

    Some ministries require consistent home group involvement. Oasis, \n our children’s program, is one example.
